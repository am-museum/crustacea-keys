Gnathophausia gracilis Willemöes-Suhm, 1875

Gnathophausia gracilis Willemöes-Suhm, 1875: 33, pl. 9, fig. 1. --Willemöes-Suhm, 1876: 576. --Sars, 1884: 9. --Sars, 1885: 48. --Wood-Mason & Alcock, 1891: 188 (G. gracilis var. brevispinis). --Gerstaecker & Ortmann, 1906: 39. --Hansen, 1912: 185–186. --Tattersall, 1922: 445. --Illig, 1930: 409. --Tattersall, 1939: 225–226. --Fage, 1941: 27, figs. 27–30. --Tattersall, 1951: 28. --Banner, 1954: 8. --Tattersall, 1955: 39. --Gordan, 1957: 333. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 57. --Bacescu, 1991: 81, fig. 1 I-K. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 426.
Gnathophausia brevispinis. --Wood-Mason & Alcock, 1891: 269. --Faxon, 1895: 216, pl. J. --Anderson, 1897: 89.
Gnathophausia dentata. --Faxon, 1893: 217.
Gnathophausia sp. --Chun, 1900: 289. --Chun, 1903: 551.
Gnathophausia bidentata. --Illig, 1906: 229: fig. 2A-D.


Type material. HOLOTYPE: male, wherabouts unknown (possibly BMNH).

Type locality. 287 km east of St. Paul's rocks, off the Algerian coast, Atlantic Ocean (20°09.6'N 17°48.9'W), 1100 m depth.

Description. Based on Willemöes-Suhm, 1875: 33, pl. 9, fig. 1.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, elongate, attenuated. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, longer than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, with spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture absent, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Body smooth. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, terminal brush of setae present.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-4 biramous. Pleopod 4 endopod with multiple articles. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, endopod and exopod subequal in length; exopod diaresis present, robust setae absent, slender setae present, on distal quarter of lateral martgin. Telson longer than broad, distally emarginate and serrated, emarginate, without plumose setae, robust setae present along distal third of outer margin.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami absent.

Australian distribution. Queensland: east of Cape York (Lowry & Stoddart, 2003).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Panama (Bacescu, 1991); Galapogas Islands (Bacescu, 1991); Californian coast (Bacescu, 1991); Indian Ocean, Madagascar, Sri Lanka (Bacescu, 1991); Pacific Ocean (Bacescu, 1991); Philippines (Bacescu, 1991); Mauritius (Bacescu, 1991).
