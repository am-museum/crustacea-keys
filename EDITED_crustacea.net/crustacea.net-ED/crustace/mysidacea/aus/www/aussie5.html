Pseudomma australe (Sars, 1883)

Ambylops australis Sars, 1883: 37. --Sars, 1884: 37. --Sars, 1885: 191, pl. 33, figs 17–18.
Pseudomma australe. --Gerstaecker & Ortmann, 1901: 602. --Hansen, 1910: 197. --Gordan, 1957: 374. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 73. --Mauchline, 1980: 330. --Fenton, 1985c: 78. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 445.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: BMNH 1886: 37.

Type locality. Off entrance to Port Phillip, Victoria, Bass Strait, Australia (33°22'S 144°26'E), 60 m depth.

Description. Based on Sars, 1883: 37.
Head. Eyes reduced, plate-like, plate parallel, contiguous, distolateral corner produced into pointed process, without visual elements, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, longer than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins with setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin pointed, distal spine absent, apical suture absent, spine on outer distal corner absent.

Habitat. Continental shelf.

Australian distribution. Victoria: Port Phillip, Bass Strait (Sars, 1885). Bass Strait: (Fenton, 1985c).

Australian geographic areas. Southern Australia.
