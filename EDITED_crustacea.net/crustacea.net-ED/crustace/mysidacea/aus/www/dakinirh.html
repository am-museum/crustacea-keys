Rhopalophthalmus dakini Tattersall, 1957

Rhopalopthalmus egregius. --Tattersall, 1940: 330.
Rhopalopthalmus dakini. --Tattersall, 1957: 93, figs 3a-l. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 75. --Fenton, 1985c: 50 (key). --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 462.

Type material. SYNTYPES: BMNH: 1957.3.28.13–16.

Type locality. Lake Illawarra, New South Wales.

Description. Based on Tattersall, 1957: 93, figs 3a-l.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, longer than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Body smooth. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, shorter than perepod 4, endopod slender, with distinct carpus and propodus, terminal brush of setae present.
Pleon. Uropod endopod diaresis present, robust setae present, with 1 in statocyst region only, slender setae present, exopod moderately longer then endopod; exopod diaresis present, robust setae absent. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, entire, without plumose setae, robust setae present along distal third of outer margin.

Habitat. Sublittoral.

Australian distribution. New South Wales: Lake Illawarra (Tattersall, 1940; Tattersall, 1957); Lane cove River (Tattersall, 1957); Port Jackson (AM); Port Stephens (AM).

Australian geographic areas. South-eastern Australia.
