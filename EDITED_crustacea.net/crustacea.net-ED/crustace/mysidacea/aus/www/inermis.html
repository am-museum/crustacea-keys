Idiomysis inermis Tattersall, 1922

Idiomysis inermis Tattersall, 1922: 489, figs 23, 24. --Illig, 1930: 598. --Greenwood & Hadley, 1982: 174, figs 1–2. --Gordan, 1957: 354. --Pillai, 1965: 1724, fig. 90. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 61. --Mauchline, 1980: 337. --Fenton, 1985c: 130. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 459.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: male, ZSI.

Type locality. Kilakarai, Gulf of Manaar, India, 0–4 m depth.

Description. Based on Tattersall, 1922: 489, figs 23, 24.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, narrowly rounded; spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale ratio of length x width 2:1 to 5:1, subequal in length to antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, spines on lateral margins absent, spines on medial margin absent, distally rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture absent, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, with carpus and propodus fused, terminal brush of setae present.
Pleon. Pleopod 1 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopod 2 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopod 3 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopod 4 biramous, endopod with a single article, endopod shorter than exopod, exopod with 2 articles, exopod second last article third last article absent. Uropod endopod robust setae absent, slender setae present, endopod and exopod subequal in length. Uropod exopod robust setae absent, slender setae on lateral margin present. Telson broader than long, distally acute or subacute, emarginate, without plumose setae, robust setae on lateral margins absent.

Habitat. Littoral, sublittoral; weeds and in association with the coelenterate Stoichactis haddoni (Saville-Kent).

Depth zones. Littoral.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Days Gutter and Dialba Passage, Moreton Bay (Greenwood & Hadley, 1982). South Australia: Edithburgh Jetty (Fenton, 1985c).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia; Southern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Gulf of Manaar, Kilakarai, India (Tattersall, 1922).
