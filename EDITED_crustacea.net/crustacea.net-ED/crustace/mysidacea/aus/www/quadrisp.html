Doxomysis quadrispinosa (Illig, 1906)

Mysis quadrispinosa Illig, 1906: 207, fig. 14.
Doxomysis pelagica Hansen, 1912: 205, pl. 3, fig. 3.
Doxomysis tattersalli Colosi, 1920: 248, pl 19, fig. 8.
Doxomysis zimmeri Colosi, 1920: 241, pl. 19, fig. 7.
Bathymysis varunae Pillai, 1963: 264. --Pillai, 1964: 31, fig. 18a. --Pillai, 1965: 1718: fig. 81.
Doxomysis quadrispinosa. --Illig, 1930: 480, figs 163–166. --Tattersall, 1951: 152–153. --Gordan, 1957: 346. --Ii, 1964: 385–386. --Pillai, 1965: 1718, fig. 80. --Pillai 1973: 102. --Taniguchi, 1974: 355, fig. 7. --Taniguchi, 1976: 355, fig. 4. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 53. --Mauchline, 1980: 332. --Fenton, 1985c: 95, 96 (key). --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 452.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: female, ZMB 22 599.

Type locality. Chagos Islands, 1900 m depth.

Description. Based on Illig, 1906: 207, fig. 14.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, triangular, acute, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale lateral margins with setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal spine absent, apical suture present.
Pleon. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, cleft or notched more than 30%, with two plumose setae, robust setae present along entire inner margin, along distal half of outer margin.

Habitat. Epipelagic.

Australian distribution. Western Australia: off north-western coast (Pillai 1973).

Australian geographic areas. North-western Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Indo West Pacific (Tattersall, 1951; Pillai 1973).
