Pseudomysidetes russelli Tattersall, 1936

Pseudomysidetes russelli Tattersall, 1936a: 157, figs 6–8. --Gordan, 1957: 376. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977; 14. --Fenton, 1985c: 111. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 455.

Type material. SYNTYPES: BMNH.

Type locality. Outside Trinity Opening, Great Barrier Reef, Queensland, Australia.

Description. Based on Tattersall, 1936a: 157, figs 6–8.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, subequal in length to antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins with setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner present.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, subequal in length to pereopod 4, endopod slender.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-4 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami triangular. Pleopod 4 endopod with a single article. Uropod endopod robust setae absent, exopod moderately longer than endopod. Telson longer than broad, distally truncate, entire, without plumose setae, robust setae present along distal third of outer margins.

Habitat. Continental slope.

Australian distribution. Queensland: outside Trininty Opening, Great Barrier Reef (Tattersall, 1936a). Bass Strait: (Fenton, 1985c).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia; South-eastern Australia.
