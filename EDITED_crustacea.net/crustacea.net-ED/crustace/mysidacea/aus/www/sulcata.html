Mysidella sulcata Murano, 2002

Mysidella sulcata Murano, 2002: 80, figs 9, 10. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 440.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: female (immature), 8.2 mm, NSMT Cr 14200. PARATYPES: male (immature), 6.6 mm, NSMT Cr 14201, 3 females (immature), 6.3, 6.2, 6.1 mm, 3 juveniles, 4.7, 4.4, 4.2 mm, NSMT Cr 14202.

Type locality. Timor Sea: north-west of Australia (12°42.2'S 123°07.6'E to 12°42.0'S 123°08.5'E), 535–547 m depth; south-east of Timor (09°27.0'S 127°58.6'E to 09°28.5'S 127°56.1'E), 610–690  m depth.

Description. Based on Murano, 2002: 80, figs 9, 10.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, narrowly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, longer than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins with setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner present. Labrum asymmetrical.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, with carpus and propodus fused and subdivided, terminal brush of setae present.
Pleon. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, 47 setae on entire medial margin, slender setae present, exopod moderately longer then endopod; exopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, on entire lateral margin. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, cleft or notched 20–30%, without plumose setae, robust setae present along entire inner margin, along distal two thirds of lateral margin.

Habitat. Benthic.

Australian distribution. Western Australia: off north-west coast (Murano, 2002).

Australian geographic areas. North-western Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Timor Sea: south-east of Timor (Murano, 2002); Sulu Sea: north of Sumbawa (Murano, 2002).
