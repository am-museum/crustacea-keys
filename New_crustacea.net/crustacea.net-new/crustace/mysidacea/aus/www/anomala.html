Siriella anomala Hansen, 1910

Siriella anomala Hansen, 1910: 41, pl. V, figs. 1a-l. --Tattersall, 1936a: 146. --Tattersall, 1936b: 279. --Tattersall, 1951: 79. --Gordan, 1957:. --Ii, 1964: 143, figs. 38–39. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 76. --Fenton, 1985c: 37, 45 (key). --Muller, 1993: 33. --Panampunnayil, 1995: 1945 (table). --Fukuoka & Murano, 1997: 523, fig. 3A-D. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 465.

Type material. SYNTYPES: 11 specimens, ZMC, many specimens, ZMA.

Material Described. Based on Hansen, 1910: 41, pl. V, figs. 1a-l.

Type locality. Sailus Ketjil, Pulau Satengar (as Paternoster Islands) Siboga Station 37; anchorage off Pulu Kawassang, Pulau Satengar (as Paternoster Islands) Siboga Station 40, Siboga Station 41 (7°25'S 117°50.5'E).

Description. Based on Hansen, 1910: 41, pl. V, figs. 1a-l.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, triangular, sub-acute. Antenna 2 scale length 6:1 to 9:1 x width, lateral margins without setae, without continuous spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, midlateral spine present, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent. Labrum with medial process.
Pleon. Pleopod 2 biramous, pseudobranchial rami straight. Pleopod 3 biramous; endopod longer than exopod. Pleopod 4 biramous; endopod with multiple articles, longer than exopod. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present on entire medial margin, slender setae present, exopod moderately longer then endopod; exopod diaresis present, 10–13 robust setae present, slender setae present, below diaresis. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, entire, with two plumose setae, robust setae present along entire outer margins.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Antenna 2 scale ratio of length x width 2:1 to 5:1, distal spine present. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami absent.

Habitat. Pelagic.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Low Isles, Great Barrier Reef (Tattersall, 1936a).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Eastern Indonesia: Pulau Satengar (as Paternoster Islands), Sunda Sea (Hansen, 1910); Marshall Islands, Caroline Islands, Philippine Islands (Tattersall, 1951); Iwayama Bay, Korror Island, Palau Islands (Ii, 1964); Ryukyu Islands, Japan (Fukuoka & Murano, 1997).
