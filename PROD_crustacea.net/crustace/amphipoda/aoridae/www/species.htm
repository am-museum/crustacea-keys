Australian Amphipoda: Aoridae

J.K. Lowry, P.B Berents & R.T. Springthorpe

? Bemlos sp.

Lembos philacanthus. --Chilton, 1921b: 77, fig. 11a-c (not L. philacanthus Stebbing, 1888: 1082, pl. 110).

Description. Based on Chilton, 1921: 77, fig. 11a-c. Head. Maxilliped basis without strong flange on anterior margin.

Peraeon. Gnathopod 1 subchelate; coxa with anteroventral corner not produced; basis anterior margin without triangular spine, without stridulating ridges, anterodistal margin with flange weak or absent, posterodistal margin with setae sparse or absent; ischium anterior margin without flange, posterior margin with dense rows of setae. Gnathopod 1 merus not greatly elongated. Gnathopod 1 posterior margin with dense rows of setae, with acute posterodistal spine; carpus anterior margin without stridulating ridges, anterior margin with setae sparse or absent. Gnathopod 1 without an oblique row of long setae on medial face. Gnathopod 1 posterior margin without spines, without posterodistal spine; propodus longer than carpus, palm present, with spine near base of dactylus, spine near base of dactylus subquadrate or subtriangular, apically subacute or rounded, margin forming a broad excavate sinus, defined by strong posterodistal spine, with robust seta defining palm; dactylus overlapping palm, dactylus more than half length of propodus. Gnathopod 2 subchelate; basis anterodistal margin without flange, posterodistal margin without robust setae; carpus anterior margin not lobate, carpus anterior margin with setae sparse or absent; propodus anterior margin sparsely setose, palm with robust seta(e) defining palm.

Pleon. Uropod 3 biramous.

Habitat. Marine.

Distribution. Tasmania: east coast; eastern slope of Bass Strait (Chilton, 1921).

Geographic areas. South-eastern Australia, or Southern Australia.

(Variant) Female (sexually dimorphic characters).

Peraeon. Gnathopod 1 posterior margin with setae sparse or absent; gnathopod 1 posterior margin with sparse setae or setae absent; without spine near base of dactylus, margin entire, defined by subquadrate corner; dactylus fitting palm, dactylus about half length of propodus.
