Australian Amphipoda: Melitidae

J.K. Lowry, P.B Berents & R.T. Springthorpe

Dulichiella anisochir (Kroyer, 1845)

Melita anisochir Kroyer, 1845: 317, pl. II, fig. 1a-p. --Dana, 1852: 968, pl. 66, fig. 8a-d. --Ledoyer, 1986: 185.

Melita australis Haswell, 1879: 264, pl. 9, figs 6-7. --Haswell, 1882: 252.

Melita fresnelii. Stebbing, 1906: 423 (in part). --Stebbing, 1910: 596-597, 642. --Chilton, 1921: 70. --Hale, 1927: 314.

Melita appendiculatus. --Stebbing, 1906: 428 (in part). --J.L. Barnard, 1970: 161, fig. 101. --J.L. Barnard, 1971: 85. --Ledoyer, 1978: 282. --Ledoyer, 1979: 86, fig. 50. --Hirayama & Kikuchi, 1979: 67, figs 2-6.

?Melita appendiculatus. --Griffiths, 1973: 286. --Griffiths, 1974a: 191. --Griffiths, 1974b: 237.

Dulichiella appendiculata. --Karaman & Barnard, 1979: 152 (in part). --Barnard & Barnard, 1983: 668 (in part).

Type material. Probably lost.

Type locality. Rio De Janiero, Brazil.

Description. Based on male, 10 mm, AM P60600 and female, AM P60601. Head lateral cephalic lobes broad, truncated, lacking notch or slit, anteroventral corner subquadrate. Antenna 1 longer than antenna 2; peduncular article 1 not geniculate with article 2, shorter than article 2, with 4 or more robust setae along posterior margin; flagellum with 40 articles (at least); accessory flagellum with 6 articles. Antenna 2 peduncular article 2 cone gland reaching at least to end of peduncular article 3; article 4 subequal to article 5; flagellum with 12 articles (about). Mandible palp present, well developed, article 3 rectolinear, setose along straight medial margin, longer than article 1, article 2 shorter than article 3, article 1 produced distally, shorter than article 2, about as long as broad. Maxilla 1 inner plate with setae mainly terminal.

Pereon. Gnathopod 1 not sexually dimorphic; coxa anteroventral corner not produced, posteroventral corner notch present; merus without posterodistal tooth; carpus about 2 x as long as broad; propodus without anterodistal projection, posterodistal margin not swollen, palm nearly transverse, convex (slightly) or straight, defined by posterodistal corner, without posterodistal robust setae. Gnathopod 2 sexually dimorphic; chelate; left and right gnathopods unequal in size; coxa posteroventral corner notch present; (larger) merus with rounded posteroventral corner; compressed; propodus, distolateral margin with 3 rounded (indistinct) teeth; palm angle obtuse, straight, posterodistal tooth absent, without robust setae; dactylus apically blunt; (smaller) merus with sharp posterventral tooth; long; palm straight, without posteroventral tooth. Pereopod 5 basis posterior margin straight, posteroventral corner narrowly rounded or subquadrate. Pereopod 6 coxa anterior lobe ventral margin slightly produced, rounded; basis posterior margin straight, basis posteroventral corner narrowly rounded or subquadrate; propodus not expanded posterodistally. Pereopod 7 basis posterior margin straight or subsigmoidal (slightly), with posterior margin smooth or minutely castelloserrate, posteroventral corner narrowly rounded or subquadrate; propodus not expanded posterodistally.

Pleon. Pleonite 1 with dorsal serrations, with dorsodistal spine, not dorsally bicarinate. Pleonite 2 with dorsal serrations, with dorsodistal spine, not dorsally bicarinate. Pleonite 3 with dorsal serrations, with dorsodistal spine, dorsally smooth. Epimeron 1 anteroventral corner without curved spine. Epimeron 1 posteroventral corner broadly rounded. Epimera 1-2 posteroventral margin without spines above posteroventral corner. Epimeron 3 posterior margin smooth, posteroventral corner with strongly produced acute spine, posteroventral margin smooth. Urosomite 1 without dorsal carina, without a small dorsal hump, without dorsal serrations, with broad dorsal midline gape, rarely bearing midline spines, dorsally bicarinate, without posterodorsal spine. Urosomite 2 posterior margin serrate, with two groups of 1-3 small dorsolateral robust setae. Urosomite 3 without dorsal robust setae. Uropod 1 peduncle with basofacial robust seta, without distoventral spur. Uropod 3 greatly exceeding uropods 1 and 2; inner ramus scale-like, much shorter than outer ramus; outer ramus long (more than 1.6 x longer than peduncle), 2-articulate. Telson cleft, without robust setae on inner margins, without apical robust setae, apical conical extension absent.

Habitat. Marine; living among bryozoans, sponges, ascidians and algae.

Depth zone. Littoral, or Continental shelf (4.5 to 120 m).

Remarks. Once LeCroy (2000) redescribed D. appendiculata (Say, 1818) from near the type locality (LeCroy, pers. comm.) it was clear that material from Australia did not belong to that species. Ledoyer (1986) gave an excellent summary of the problems associated with available names for species of Dulichiella. Based on LeCroy's (2000) redescription, Ledoyer's (1986) essay and the available literature, there appears to be two available names for the species with three distolateral blunt teeth on the male large propodus of gnathopod 2, Dulichiella anisochir (Kroyer, 1845) from Rio de Janiero, Brazil and Dulichiella australis (Haswell, 1879) from Port Jackson, Australia. The only differences we can see between the illustrations of these species is that antennae 1 and 2 appear to be subequal in D. anisochir and the tip of the dactylus of the male large gnathopod 2 is subacute, not blunt as in D. australis. Dulichiella anisochir has priority and therefore becomes the the valid name for the species. We illustrate it here based on material from eastern Australia. Material from Hawaii has been well illustrated by J.L. Barnard (1970); from Japan by Hirayama & Kikuchi (1979); from New Caledonia by Ledoyer (1986); and from Madagascar and Mauritius by Ledoyer (1979; 1983). Dulichiella from southern Africa has never been illustrated, but Griffiths (1973; 1974a; 1974b) reported a Dulichiella with three distolateral teeth on the propodus of gnathopod 2. This corresponds with D. anisochir, but until the species is illustrated it cannot be identified.

Distribution. Queensland: Lizard Island (AM). New South Wales: Julian Rocks, Byron Bay; Mary's Rock, Cook Island; Split Solitary Island; Coff's Harbour; Boambee Creek, Sawtell (all AM); Tacking Point (Haswell, 1879); Manning River (Stebbing, 1910); Port Stephens; off Burwood Beach; Box Head, Broken Bay; off Long Reef (all AM); Port Jackson (Haswell, 1879); Clark Island, Port Jackson; Botany Bay; off Wattamolla; off Wollongong; Jervis Bay; Twofold Bay (all AM). Victoria: Western Port (NMV). Tasmania: Esperance Point, D'Entrecasteaux Channel (AM). South Australia: Sanders Bank, Kangaroo Island (AM); Spencers Gulf (Chilton, 1921).

Extrinsic distribution. Rio de Janiero, Brazil; Hawaii; Japan; New Caledonia; Madagascar and Mauritius.

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia, or South-eastern Australia, or Southern Australia.

(Variant) Female (sexually dimorphic characters).

Pereon. Gnathopod 2 subchelate; left and right gnathopods subequal in size; merus with sharp posteroventral tooth; carpus short; propodus without medial depression, palm acute (slightly), straight or concave (slightly), smooth, without robust setae, defined by posterodistal robust setae, defined by posteroventral corner; apically acute/subacute. Pereopod 7 basis posterior margin straight.
