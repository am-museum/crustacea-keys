Australian Amphipoda: Melitidae

J.K. Lowry, P.B Berents & R.T. Springthorpe

Dulichiella sp.

Dulichiella appendiculata. --Berents, 1983: 111, fig. 9. --Ledoyer, 1986: 187, fig. 9S.

Dulichiella fresneli. --Kunkel, 1910: 31, fig. 11.

Dulichiella sp. A LeCroy, 2000: 78, fig. 126.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: male "a", 4.4 mm, AM P61112. PARATYPES: female, 5.8 mm, AM P61113; male "b", AM P61114; 4 males, AM P61115; 1 male, AM P61116; 1 male, 5 ovigerous females, 3 non-ovigerous females, AM P61117.

Type locality. Between Tandai Point & Koilo Point, Guadalcanal, Solomon Islands (9°22.5'S 159° 52.2'E), coral rubble.

Description. Based on holotype male "a", AM P61112, paratype female, AM P61113, and paratype male "b", AM P61114. Head lateral cephalic lobes broad, truncated, lacking notch or slit, anteroventral corner subquadrate. Antenna 1 longer than antenna 2 (slightly); peduncular article 1 not geniculate with article 2, shorter than article 2, with 3 robust setae along posterior margin; flagellum with 28 articles (about); accessory flagellum with 4 articles. Antenna 2 peduncular article 2 cone gland reaching at least to end of peduncular article 3; article 4 subequal to article 5; flagellum with 12 articles. Mandible palp present, well developed, article 3 rectolinear, setose along straight medial margin, longer than article 1, article 2 subequal to article 3, article 1 produced distally, shorter than article 2, about as long as broad. Maxilla 1 inner plate with setae mainly terminal.

Pereon. Gnathopod 1 not sexually dimorphic; coxa anteroventral corner not produced, posteroventral corner notch present; merus without posterodistal tooth; carpus about 2 x as long as broad; propodus without anterodistal projection, posterodistal margin not swollen, palm acute, convex, without posterodistal corner, defined by posterodistal robust setae. Gnathopod 2 sexually dimorphic; chelate; left and right gnathopods unequal in size; coxa posteroventral corner notch present; (larger) merus with rounded posteroventral corner; compressed; propodus, distolateral margin with 4 acute (distinct) teeth; palm angle obtuse, straight, posterodistal tooth absent, without robust setae; dactylus apically blunt; (smaller) merus with sharp posterventral tooth; long; palm straight, without posteroventral tooth. Pereopod 5 basis posterior margin straight, posteroventral corner narrowly rounded or subquadrate. Pereopod 6 coxa anterior lobe ventral margin slightly produced, rounded; basis posterior margin concave (slightly), basis posteroventral corner narrowly rounded or subquadrate; propodus not expanded posterodistally. Pereopod 7 basis posterior margin straight, with posterior margin smooth or minutely castelloserrate, posteroventral corner narrowly rounded or subquadrate; propodus not expanded posterodistally.

Pleon. Pleonite 1 with dorsal serrations, with dorsodistal spine, not dorsally bicarinate. Pleonite 2 with dorsal serrations, with dorsodistal spine, not dorsally bicarinate. Pleonite 3 with dorsal serrations, with dorsodistal spine, dorsally smooth. Epimeron 1 anteroventral corner without curved spine. Epimeron 1 posteroventral corner with small acute spine. Epimera 1-2 posteroventral margin without spines above posteroventral corner. Epimeron 3 posterior margin smooth, posteroventral corner with strongly produced acute spine, posteroventral margin smooth. Urosomite 1 without dorsal carina, without a small dorsal hump, without dorsal serrations, with broad dorsal midline gape, rarely bearing midline spines, dorsally bicarinate, without posterodorsal spine. Urosomite 2 posterior margin serrate, with two groups of 1-3 small dorsolateral robust setae. Urosomite 3 without dorsal robust setae. Uropod 1 peduncle with basofacial robust seta, without distoventral spur. Uropod 3 greatly exceeding uropods 1 and 2; inner ramus scale-like, much shorter than outer ramus; outer ramus long (more than 1.6 x longer than peduncle), 2-articulate. Telson cleft, without robust setae on inner margins, without apical robust setae, apical conical extension absent.

Habitat. Marine; coral rubble, sponges, sand with low algal turf.

Depth zone. Littoral (10 to 20 m).

Remarks. This distinctive species has been previously been illustrated from Senegal by Ledoyer (1986) and by LeCroy (2000) from Florida. It is common in Singapore and occurs along the archipelago which runs from New Guinea to Fiji. It is currently being described (Lowry & Springthorpe, in prep.).

Distribution. Queensland: Yonge Reef, Great Barrier Reef; Lizard Island (Berents, 1983; AM).

Extrinsic distribution. Senegal, Singapore, New Guinea, Solomon Islands, New Caledonia, Gulf of Mexico, Bermuda.

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia.

(Variant) Female (sexually dimorphic characters).

Pereon. Gnathopod 2 subchelate; left and right gnathopods subequal in size; merus with sharp posteroventral tooth; carpus long; propodus without medial depression, palm acute, concave, smooth, with sparse robust setae, defined by posterodistal robust setae, defined by posteroventral corner; apically acute/subacute. Pereopod 7 basis posterior margin subsigmoidal.
