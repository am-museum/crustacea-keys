Urohaustorius parnggius Barnard & Drummond, 1982

Urohaustorius parnggius Barnard & Drummond, 1982: 74, figs 25, 26.

Type Material. Lodged in the NMV.

Type Locality. Western Entrance, Western Port, Victoria, Australia.

Description. Antenna 1 peduncular article 1 setal row with some strong robust setae or with only slender setae, proximal pair of setae simple; accessory flagellum 2 -articulate; primary flagellum 14–15 -articulate. Antenna 2 peduncular article 4 main setal row with robust and slender setae, proximal part of row displaced ventrally; peduncle article 5 bulbous in male. Mandible right incisor simple. Maxilla 1 palp with some setae displaced subapically.
Gnathopod 1 simple; coxa small, subequal in size to coxa 2. Gnathopod 2 minutely chelate, propodus palm obtuse. Pereopod 3 coxa large, larger than coxa 4. Pereopod 4 coxa large. Pereopod 5 dactylus with robust setae arranged in single row. Pereopod 6 dactylus well developed. Pereopod 7 basis fully expanded; dactylus well developed.
Epimeron 2 subequal in size to epimeron 3, posteroventral corner with small spine, more setose than epimeron 3. Epimeron 3 posteroventral corner with small spine. Uropod 1 inner ramus shorter than outer ramus, not fused to peduncle. Uropod 2 inner ramus shorter than outer ramus, not fused to peduncle. Telson longer than broad, entire.

Distribution. Australia: Western Port, Victoria to southern New South Wales.
