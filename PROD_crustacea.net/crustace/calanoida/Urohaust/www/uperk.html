Urohaustorius perkeus Barnard & Drummond, 1982

Urohaustorius perkeus Barnard & Drummond, 1982: 85, figs 28, 29.

Type Material. Lodged in the NMV.

Type Locality. Western Port Bay, Victoria.

Description. Antenna 1 peduncular article 1 setal row with only slender setae, proximal pair of setae plumose; accessory flagellum 6 -articulate; primary flagellum 8 -articulate. Antenna 2 peduncular article 4 main setal row with only slender setae; peduncle article 5 not bulbous in male. Mandible right incisor simple; left incisor with 2 teeth. Maxilla 1 palp with some setae displaced subapically.
Gnathopod 1 simple; coxa small, subrectangular, subequal in size to coxa 2. Gnathopod 2 minutely chelate, propodus palm obtuse. Pereopod 3 coxa large, larger than coxa 4. Pereopod 4 coxa large, posteroventral lobe absent. Pereopod 5 basis without mediofacial brush of setae; dactylus with robust setae. Pereopod 6 basis fully expanded; dactylus vestigial. Pereopod 7 coxa without long posterior spine; basis fully expanded, with slender setae along posteroproximal margin; dactylus vestigial.
Epimeron 2 subequal in size to epimeron 3, posteroventral corner with large spine, more setose than epimeron 3. Epimeron 3 posteroventral corner with large spine. Uropod 1 inner ramus shorter than outer ramus, not fused to peduncle. Uropod 2 inner ramus shorter than outer ramus, not fused to peduncle. Telson moderately cleft.

Distribution. Australia: Western Port Bay, Victoria to Moreton Bay, Queensland.
