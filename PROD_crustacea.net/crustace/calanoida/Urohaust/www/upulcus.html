Urohaustorius pulcus Barnard & Drummond, 1982

Urohaustorius pulcus Barnard & Drummond, 1982: 53, figs 19–23.

Type Material. Lodged in the NMV.

Type Locality. Western Port, Victoria, Australia.

Description. Antenna 1 peduncular article 1 setal row with some strong robust setae, proximal pair of setae simple; accessory flagellum 8 -articulate; primary flagellum 10–12 -articulate. Antenna 2 peduncular article 4 main setal row with robust and slender setae, proximal part of row evenly angled; peduncle article 5 not bulbous in male. Mandible right incisor simple; left incisor with 2 teeth. Maxilla 1 palp with some setae displaced subapically.
Gnathopod 1 simple; coxa small, triangular, subequal in size to coxa 2. Gnathopod 2 minutely chelate, propodus palm obtuse. Pereopod 3 coxa large, larger than coxa 4. Pereopod 4 coxa large. Pereopod 5 basis without mediofacial brush of setae; dactylus with robust setae. Pereopod 6 basis fully expanded; merus with slender setae on posterior margin continuous to apex; dactylus well developed. Pereopod 7 coxa without long posterior spine; basis fully expanded; dactylus well developed, without robust setae.
Epimeron 2 subequal in size to epimeron 3, posteroventral corner with small spine, more setose than epimeron 3. Epimeron 3 posteroventral corner with small spine, or narrowly rounded. Uropod 1 inner ramus shorter than outer ramus, not fused to peduncle. Uropod 2 inner ramus shorter than outer ramus, not fused to peduncle. Telson subequal in length and breadth, weakly to moderately cleft.

Habitat. Sand-shell bottoms.

Distribution. Australia: Port Phillip Bay, Victoria to Port Macquarie, New South Wales.
