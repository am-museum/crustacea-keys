Australomysis acuta Tattersall, 1927

Australomysis acuta Tattersall, 1927: 249, figs 102–103. --Hale, 1929: 359. --Illig, 1930: 583. --Gordan, 1957: 341. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 49. --Fenton, 1985c: 88 (key). --Fenton, 1992: 177. --Mauchline, 1980: 332. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 451.

Type material. SYNTYPES: SAMA, whereabouts unknown (possibly in BMNH).

Type locality. 10 km off Semaphore, Gulf St Vincent, South Australia, 10–13 m depth.

Description. Based on Tattersall, 1927: 249, figs 102–103.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, triangular, acute, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, lateral margins with setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, apical suture present.
Pereon. Body smooth. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, terminal brush of setae absent.
Pleon. Pleopod 1 biramous. Pleopod 2-4 biramous. Pleopod 4 endopod with multiple articles, endopod shorter than exopod; exopod with 6 articles, second last article subequal to third last article. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, 22 setae from statocyst nearly to the apex of medial margin, slender setae present, exopod moderately longer than endopod; exopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present on entire lateral margin. Telson longer than broad, distally truncate, cleft or notched 20-30%, without plumose setae, robust setae present along entire inner and outer margins.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopod 2-4 uniramous or rudimentary.  Pleopod 4 endopod with a single article.

Habitat. Continental shelf, soft bottom.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Middle Banks, Moreton Bay (QM). Bass Strait (Fenton, 1985c). Tasmania: Partridge Island; Fortescue Bay, Tasman Peninsula; Margate Beach; One Tree Point, Bruny Island (Fenton, 1985c). South Australia: Gulf St Vincent (Tattersall, 1927); near Port Pirie and Outer Harbour (Fenton, 1985c). Western Australia: Warnbro Sound (AM).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia, Southern Australia, South-western Australia.
