Siriella affinis Hansen, 1910

Siriella affinis Hansen, 1910: 37, pl. III, fig. 3a-i. --Tattersall, 1922: 454. --Illig, 1930: 562. --Tattersall, 1951: 64. --Gordan, 1957: 378. --Ii, 1964: 89. --Pillai, 1965: 1692, fig. 16. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 76. --Murano, 1983: 82. --Fenton, 1985c: 37, 45 (key). --Carleton & Hamner, 1989: 464, table 1. --Muller, 1993: 32. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 465.

Type material. SYNTYPES: 5 specimens, ZMC, 20 specimens, ZMA.

Type locality. Bank between the Islands of Bahuluwang and Tambolugan, south of Saleyer, 8–10 m depth; anchorage off Pulau Tongil, Sulu Archipelago, 13 m depth, Indonesia.

Description. Based on Hansen, 1910: 37, pl. III, fig. 3a-i.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, triangular, acute or subacute, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, shorter than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent. Labrum with medial process.
Pereon. Body smooth.
Pleon. Pleopod 2-4 biramous. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami spirally coiled. Pleopod 3 subequal to exopod. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present from statocyst to middle part of medial margin, slender setae present, endopod and exopod subequal in length; exopod diaresis present, 6–7 robust setae present, slender setae present below diaresis. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, entire, without plumose setae, robust setae present along entire outer margins.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami absent.

Habitat. Coral reef, pelagic.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Davies Reef, Great Barrier Reef (Carleton & Hamner, 1989); Heron Island, Great Barrier Reef (Fenton, 1985c).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Eastern Indonesia (Hansen, 1910); Sulu Archipelago (Hansen, 1910); Philippine Islands (Tattersall, 1951).
