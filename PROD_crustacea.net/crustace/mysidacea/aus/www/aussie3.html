Notomysis australiensis (Tattersall, 1927)

Leptomysis australiensis Tattersall, 1927: 246, fig. 100. --Hale, 1929: 358. --Illig, 1930: 582. --Gordan, 1957: 355. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 62. --Fenton, 1985c: 103.
Leptomysis sp. --?Dakin & Colefax, 1940: 135.
Leptomysis longisquama Panamunnayil, 1986: 1189, figs 27–47.
Notomysis australiensis. --Wittman, 1986: 139. --Fenton, 1992: 177. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 454.

Type material. SYNTYPES: SAMA C1617.

Type locality. 10 km off Semaphore, 3 km off Semaphore, Gulf St Vincent, South Australia, 9–13 m.

Description. Based on Tattersall, 1927: 246, fig. 100.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, narrowly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 6.1 to 9:1 x width, lateral margins with setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, terminal brush of setae absent.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-4 biramous. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami straight. Pleopod 4 endopod with multiple articles, endopod shorter than exopod; exopod with 10 articles, second last article subequal to third last article. Uropod endopod robust setae present, 7 setae from statocyst to middle part of medial margin, exopod moderately longer than endopod; slender setae present, below diaresis. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, emarginate, without plumose setae, robust setae present along entire outer margins.
Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopods 1-4 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami triangular.

Habitat. Continental shelf, sublittoral.

Remarks. Fenton (1985c) considered that specimens of a new species reported by Dakin & Colefax (1940) from Broken Bay, New South Wales, were probably Notomysis australiensis, but her attempts to locate the specimens and confirm the identification were unsuccessful.

Australian distribution. New South Wales: Broken Bay (Dakin & Colefax, 1940). Bass Strait (Fenton, 1985c). Tasmania: Partridge Island; Southerly Bight; Blow Hole, Tasman Peninsula; One Tree Point, Bruny Island (Fenton, 1985c). South Australia: Gulf St Vincent (Tattersall, 1927); between Reevsby and Partney Islands (Fenton, 1985c); Edithburgh Jetty (Fenton, 1985c). Western Australia: south-west coast between Busselton and Albany (Panampunnayil, 1986).

Australian geographic areas. South-eastern Australia; Southern Australia; South-western Australia.
