Doxomysis australiensis (Tattersall, 1940)

Afromysis australiensis Tattersall, 1940: 336, figs 5–6. --Dakin & Colefax, 1940: 134. --Gordan, 1957: 338. --Tattersall, 1958: 383.
Doxomysis australiensis. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 53. --Mauchline, 1980: 332. --Bacescu & Udrescu, 1982: 91, fig. 5A-I. --Fenton, 1985c: 95 (key). --Talbot, 1997: 434. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 451.

Type material. SYNTYPES: 50 specimens, AM P11169.

Type locality. Broken Bay, New South Wales, Australia.

Description. Based on Tattersall, 1940: 336, figs 5–6.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, narrowly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 6:1 to 9:1 x width, longer than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins with setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner present.
Pereon. Body smooth. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, with carpus and propodus fused and subdivided, terminal brush of setae absent.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-4 biramous. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami straight. Pleopod 4 endopod with multiple articles, endopod shorter than exopod; exopod with 6 articles, second last article slightly longer than third last article. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, 48 setae from statocyst nearly to the apex of medial margin, slender setae present, exopod much longer than endopod; exopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, on entire lateral margin. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, cleft or notched more than 30%, with two plumose setae, robust setae present along entire inner and outer margins.
Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopods 1-4 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami triangular. Pleopod 4 endopod with a single article.

Habitat. Continental shelf, soft bottom.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Lizard Island, Great Barrier Reef (Talbot, 1997); Middle Banks, Toorbul Point, Moreton Bay (Bacescu & Udrescu, 1982). New South Wales: Broken Bay (Tattersall, 1940; Dakin & Colefax, 1940). Bass Strait (Lowry & Stoddart, 2003).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia, South-eastern Australia, Southern Australia.
