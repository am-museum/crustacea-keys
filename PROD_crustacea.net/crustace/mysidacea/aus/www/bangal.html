Haplostylus bengalensis (Hansen, 1910)

Gastrosaccus bengalensis Hansen, 1910: 1–123, 16 pls. --Zimmer, 1915: 164. --Tattersall, 1922: 462. --Illig, 1930: 568. --Tattersall, 1952: 176. --Gordan, 1957: 1957. --Ii, 1964: 256, figs 67–68. --Pillai, 1965: 1699. --Tattersall, 1965: 81. --Pillai, 1973: 59, figs 26–27. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 55). --Bacescu & Udrescu, 1982: 88. --Murano, 1983: 82. --Bacescu, 1986: 19. --Panumpunnayil, 1989: 1313 (table). --Wang & Liu, 1994: 208.
Gastrosaccus bengalis. --Zimmer, 1918: 15.
Gastrosaccus philippinensis Tattersall, 1951: 90, fig. 25.
Haplostylus (Gastrosaccus) bengalensis. --Fenton, 1985c: 56, 61 (key).
Haplostylus bengalensis. --Fenton, 1990b: 449 (key). --Greenwood et al., 1991: 521 (table). --Fukuoka & Murano, 1997: 525. --Fukuoka & Murano, 2002: 64, 100 (key). --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 434.

Type material. SYNTYPES: many specimens, ZMC.

Type locality. Bay of Bengal.

Description. Based on Hansen, 1910: 1–123, 16 pls.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, triangular, acute, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, shorter than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent. Labrum with medial process; medial process without setae.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, shorter than perepod 4, endopod slender, with carpus and propodus fused and subdivided, terminal brush of setae present.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-4 biramous. Pleopod 3 endopod shorter than exopod. Pleopod 4 endopod with a single article, endopod shorter than exopod; exopod with 2 articles, second last article third last article absent. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, 5 setae from statocyst nearly to the apex of medial margin, slender setae present, endopod and exopod subequal in length; exopod diaresis absent, 12–13 robust setae present, slender setae absent on lateral margin. Telson longer than broad, distally angled and convex, cleft or notched less than 20%, without plumose setae, robust setae present along entire inner and outer margins.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopods 1-4 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami triangular.

Habitat. Pools on sandy coral reef.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Middle Banks, Moreton Bay (Bacescu & Udrescu, 1982). Northern Territory: Channel Island, Darwin Harbour (Bacescu, 1986).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia; Northern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Indian Ocean; Bay of Bengal (Hansen, 1910); Port Blair, Andaman Islands (Tattersall, 1922) Andaman Sea (Pillai, 1973; Fukuoka & Murano, 2002); between Sri Lanka and New Guinea (Zimmer, 1915); Strait of Malacca (Tattersall, 1965); South China Sea (Ii, 1964); Philippines (Tattersall, 1951); East China Sea (Wang & Liu, 1994); Taiwan (Zimmer, 1917); Enewetak Lagoon, Micronesia (Murano, 1983); Ryukyu Islands, Japan (Fukuoka & Murano, 1997).
