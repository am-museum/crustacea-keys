Anchialina dentata Pillai, 1964

Anchialina dentata Pillai, 1964: 19, fig. 11. --Pillai, 1965: 1702, figs. 42–45. --Pillai, 1973: 70, figs 33–34. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 46, tab. 1. --Murano, 1990: 195. --Fukuoka & Murano, 2002: 99 (key).
Anchialina parva Ii, 1964: 196, figs 50–51. --Tattersall, 1965: 83. --Fenton, 1985c: 54 (key). --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 432.

Type material. SYNTYPES: 2 males, 4 females, CMRFI.

Type locality. Five localities in the Indian Ocean, south-west of India (11°14'N 75°00' E, 11°07'N 75°22'E, 10°34'N 75°31'E, 8°17'N 76°50'E, 11°04'N 75°11'E).

Description. Based on Pillai, 1964: 19, fig. 11.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, triangular, acute or narrowly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, subequal in length to antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner present. Labrum with medial process.
Pereon. Body with extensive spination. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, terminal brush of setae present.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-3 biramous. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami circular. Pleopod 3 endopod shorter than exopod. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, on entire medial margin, slender setae present, endopod and exopod subequal in length; exopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae absent on lateral margin. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, cleft or notched 20–30%, without plumose setae, robust setae present along entire inner margin, along distal two thirds of lateral margin.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Rostrum triangular, acute. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami absent.

Habitat. Shallow water dweller.

Australian distribtion. Western Australia: west coast (Pillai, 1973).

Australian geographic areas. North-western Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Indian Ocean: south-west of India (Pillai, 1964; 1973). South China Sea: west coast of Borneo; Java Sea; Gulf of Tonkin (Ii, 1964).
