Megalopsis denticauda Panampunnayil, 1987

Megalopsis denticauda Panampunnayil, 1987: 586, figs 24–42.--Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 453.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: male, IOBC 0387–10–50–1985. PARATYPES: 1 female, IOBC 0387A-10–50–1985; 6 specimens, IOBC 0387B-10–50–1985.

Type locality. South-west coast of Australia between Busselton and Albany (34° - 35°S 114° - 119°E).

Description. Based on Panampunnayil, 1987: 586, figs 24–42.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, narrowly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 10:1 to 15:1 x width, longer than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins with setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner present.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, shorter than perepod 4, endopod slender, with carpus and propodus fused and subdivided, terminal brush of setae present.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-4 biramous. Pleopod 3 endopod shorter than exopod. Pleopod 4 endopod with multiple articles, endopod shorter than exopod; exopod with 10 articles, second last article subequal to third last article. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, 36–38 setae from statocyst nearly to the apex of medial margin, slender setae present, exopod much longer than endopod; exopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, below diaresis. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, emarginate, with two plumose setae, robust setae present along entire outer margins.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopods 1-4 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopod 4 endopod with a single article.

Habitat. Continental shelf.

Australian distribution. Western Australia: south-west coast between Busselton and Albany (Panampunnayil, 1997).

Australian geographic areas. North-western Australia.
