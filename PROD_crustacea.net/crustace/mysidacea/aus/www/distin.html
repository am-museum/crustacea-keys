Siriella distinguenda Hansen, 1910

Siriella distinguenda Hansen, 1910: 42, pl. V, figs 2a-e. --Illig, 1930: 564 (key). --Tattersall, 1943: 66. --Tattersall, 1951: 79. --Gordan, 1957: 379. --Ii, 1964: 142. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 76. Bacescu, 1979: 143. --Fenton, 1985c: 37, 46 (key). --Muller, 1993: 36. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 466.

Type material. SYNTYPES: 6 specimens, ZMA.

Type locality. Sailus ketjil, Pulau Satengar (as Paternoster Islands), 27 m depth; north of Papua New Guinea, (7°25'S 17°50.5'E), 96 m depth; anchorage off Sawan, Siau Island.

Description. Based on Hansen, 1910: 42, pl.5, fig. 2.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, triangular, acute. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, lateral margins without setae, with spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent. Labrum with medial process.
Pleon. Pleopods 3-3 biramous. Pleopod 3 endopod subequal to exopod. Pleopod 4 endopod with multiple articles, endopod longer than exopod; exopod with 10 articles, second last article subequal to third last article. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present on entire medial margin, slender setae present, exopod moderately longer then endopod; exopod diaresis present, 10–12 robust setae present, slender setae present, on distal quarter of lateral martgin. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, entire, with two plumose setae.

Habitat. Pelagic, sublittoral, coral reef.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Heron Island, Great Barrier Reef (Bacescu, 1979; Fenton, 1985).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Eastern Indonesia: Pulau Satengar (as Paternoster Islands) (Hansen, 1910); northern Papua New Guinea (Hansen, 1910); Phillippine Islands (Tattersall, 1951).
