Siriella dubia Hansen, 1910

Siriella dubia Hansen, 1910: 44, pl. V, fig. 4a-e. --Colosi, 1920: 236, pl. 18, fig. 1a. --Tattersall, 1922: 455, fig. 5a-b. --Illig, 1930: 560. --Tattersall, 1936a: 146, fig. 1. --Tattersall, 1936b: 279. --Tattersall, 1951: 79. --Gordan, 1957: 379. --Ii, 1964: 131, fig. 35. --Pillai, 1964: 7, fig. 2. --Pillai, 1965: 1688, fig. 8. --Tattersall, 1965: 77. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 76. --Fenton, 1985c: 37, 46 (key). --Muller, 1993: 36. --Wang & Liu, 1994: 76, fig. 7. --Fukuoka & Murano, 2002: 58. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 466.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: 1 female ZMA.

Type locality. Anchorage off Laiwui, Palau Obi, 23 m depth.

Description. Based on Hansen, 1910: 44, pl. 5, fig. 4.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, broadly rounded. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, subequal in length to antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner present.
Pleon. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami spirally coiled. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, on entire medial margin, slender setae present, exopod moderately longer then endopod; exopod diaresis present, 3 robust setae present, slender setae present, below diaresis. Telson longer than broad, distally concave, emarginate, with two plumose setae, robust setae along entire outer margins with a median gap.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami absent.

Habitat. Pelagic, sublittoral.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Low Isles, Great Barrier Reef (Tattersall, 1936a).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Arabian Sea (Pillai, 1964); India (Tattersall, 1922); Andaman Islands (Tattersall, 1922); Andaman Sea (Fukuoka & Murano, 2002); Strait of Malacca (Tattersall, 1965); South China Sea (Ii, 1964; Wang & Liu, 1994); Philippine Islands (Tattersall, 1951); Palau Obi, eastern Indonesia (Hansen, 1910).
