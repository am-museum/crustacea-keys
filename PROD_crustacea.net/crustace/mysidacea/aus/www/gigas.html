Neognathophausia gigas (Willemöes-Suhm, 1875)

Gnathophausia gigas Willemöes-Suhm, 1873: 400. --Willemöes-Suhm, 1874: p. iii. --Humbert, 1874: 206. --Willemöes-Suhm, 1875: 28. --Willemöes-Suhm, 1876: 576. --Sars, 1884: 5. --Sars, 1885: 33, pl. 3, figs 1–7. --Stebbing, 1893: 260. --Gerstaecker & Ortmann, 1901: 602. --Ortmann, 1906: 968. --Ortmann, 1906: 36, pl. 2, figs 1a-b. --Ortmann, 1908: 1. --Zimmer, 1909: 33. --Fowler, 1912: 539. --Tattersall, 1914: 868. --Hansen, 1927: 17. --Illig, 1930: 555. --Wolff & Krausse, 1930: 123. --Stephenson, 1933: 9. --Tattersall, 1933: 5. --Tattersall, 1939: 225. --Fage, 1941: 24, fig. 26. --Nouvel, 1943: 12. --Banner, 1948: 357, pl. 1, fig 1a-c. --Tattersall, 1951: 26.--Tattersall & Tattersall, 1951: 77, figs. 1A-D, 2A-D.--Tattersall, 1955: 36, tab. 2. --Gordan, 1957: 332.--Birstein & Tchindonova, 1962: 58. --Pequegnat, 1965: 408, fig. 5. --Taniguchi, 1969: 44, fig. 2a-c. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 57. --Lagardère, 1983: 810. --Katman, et al., 1986: 158. --Laubitz, 1986: 14.
Gnathophausia sp. Smith, 1884: 55. --Verrill, 1885: 556.
Gnathophausia drepanephora Holt & Tattersall, 1905: 113, figs 1–7. --Holt & Tattersall, 1905: 9. --Ortmann, 1906: 38. --Zimmer, 1909: 33. --Illig, 1930: 555.
Neognathophausia gigas. --Petryashov, 1992, 47, figs. 1–5. --Muller, 1993: 5. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 426.

Type material. Type status unknown.

Type locality. 644 km west of Açores, North Eastern Atlantic Ocean.

Description. Based on Willemöes-Suhm, 1873: 400, figs 4,5.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions; rostrum present, elongate, attenuated; spines on lateral margins present. Antenna 2 scale ratio of length x width 2:1 to 5:1, lateral margins without setae, spines on lateral margins present, spines on medial margin absent, distally pointed, distal spine present, apical suture absent, spine on outer distal corner present.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, with distinct carpus and propodus, terminal brush of setae absent.
Pleon. Pleopod 4 endopod with multiple articles. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, endopod and exopod subequal in length. Uropod exopod diaresis present, robust setae absent, slender setae on lateral margin present, slender setae on lateral margin below diaresis. Telson longer than broad, distally concave, entire, without plumose setae, robust setae on lateral margins present, on distal two thirds of lateral margin.

Depth zones. Lower bathyal to Upper abyssal.

Australian distribution. 85–135 km east of Sydney, Tasman Sea (Lowry & Stoddart, 2003).

Australian geographic areas. South-eastern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. 644 km west of Açores, North Eastern Atlantic Ocean (Tattersall, 1951); Ireland (Tattersall, 1951); east coast of America (Tattersall, 1951); Indian Ocean (Tattersall, 1951); Pacific Ocean, near Hawaii (Tattersall, 1951); Greenland (Tattersall, 1951).
