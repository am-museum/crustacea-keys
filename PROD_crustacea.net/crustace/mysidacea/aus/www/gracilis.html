Anisomysis gracilis Panampunnayil, 1984

Anisomysis gracilis Panampunnayil, 1984: 943, figs 1–18. --Murano, 1990: 199–200, fig. 11. --Fenton, 1985c: 124, 127 (key). --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 457.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: male, IOBC -0384–10–50–1983. PARATYPES: 1 female, IOBC 0384A-10–50–1983, 3 males & 3 females, IOBC 0384B-10–50–1983.

Type locality. South-west coast of Australia between Busselton and Pt. Hood (33º14´S 35º16´S - 114º28´E, 119º29´E).

Description. Based on Panampunnayil, 1984: 943, figs 1–18.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, triangular, acute, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 10:1 to 15:1 x width, longer than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins with setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent. Labrum symmetrical.
Pereon. Body smooth. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, shorter than perepod 4, endopod slender, with carpus and propodus fused, terminal brush of setae present.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-3 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami triangular. Pleopod 4 biramous; endopod with a single article, endopod shorter than exopod; exopod with 4 articles, second last article shorter than third last article, second last article slightly shorter than third last article. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, exopod moderately longer then endopod; exopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, below diaresis. Telson longer than broad, distally concave, entire, without plumose setae, robust setae present along distal half of outer margin.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopod 4 uniramous or greatly reduced.

Habitat. Continental shelf, pelagic.

Australian distribution. Western Australia: south-west coast between Busselton and Pt. Hood (Panumpunnayil, 1984).

Australian geographic areas. South-western Australia.
