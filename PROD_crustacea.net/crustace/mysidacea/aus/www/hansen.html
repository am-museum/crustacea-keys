Siriella hanseni Tattersall, 1922

Siriella hanseni Tattersall, 1922: 448, figs 1a-c, 2. --Illig, 1930: 561 (key). --Gordan, 1957: 379. --Tattersall, 1960: 167. --Ii, 1964: 577. --Pillai, 1965: 1689. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 76. --Bacescu, 1986: 19. --Muller, 1993: 37. --Panampunnayil, 1995: 1949 (table). --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 467.

Type material. SYNTYPES: BMNH 1964.1.21:6186–6190.

Type locality. Pamban, Gulf of Mannar, India, 0–4 m depth.

Description. Based on Tattersall, 1922: 448, figs 1a-c, 2.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, triangular, acute, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, shorter than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture absent, spine on outer distal corner present.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, with carpus and propodus fused, terminal brush of setae absent.
Pleon. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami spirally coiled. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, 10 setae from statocyst nearly to the apex of medial margin, slender setae present, exopod moderately longer then endopod; exopod diaresis present, 3–4 robust setae present, slender setae present, on distal quarter of lateral martgin. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, entire, with two plumose setae, robust setae along entire outer margins with a median gap.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Antenna 2 scale subequal in length to antenna 1 peduncle.

Habitat. Seagrass; pools on sandy coral reef.

Australian distribution. Someplace in northern Australia (Bacescu, 1986).

Extrinsic distribution. Gulf of Mannar, India (Tattersall, 1922).
