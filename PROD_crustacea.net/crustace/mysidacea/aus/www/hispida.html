Anisomysis hispida Pillai, 1973

Anisomysis hispida Pillai, 1973: 110, figs 62, 63. --Mauchline, 1980: 336. --Fenton, 1985c: 124, 127 (key). --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 457.

Type material. SYNTYPES: CMFRI.

Type locality. Mid-Indian Ocean (12°44'S, 82°57'E); mid-Bay of Bengal (15°06'N 85°08'E); mid-Andaman Sea (07°27'N 95°18'E); eastern Indian Ocean (20°00'S 110°00'E); eastern Indian Ocean (14°00'S 110°00'E).

Description. Based on Pillai, 1973: 110, figs 62, 63.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, triangular, sub-acute, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 6.1 to 9:1 x width, longer than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins with setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Body with extensive spination.
Pleon. Pleopod 4 biramous; endopod with a single article, endopod shorter than exopod; exopod with 4 articles, second last article shorter than third last article, second last article slightly shorter than third last article. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, endopod and exopod subequal in length; exopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, below diaresis. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, emarginate, without plumose setae, robust setae present along distal third of outer margin.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopod 4 uniramous or greatly reduced.

Habitat. Oceanic, pelagic.

Remarks. The closest record of A. hispida occurring near Australia is 20°S 110°E, just outside of Australian waters. However, it is reasonable to expect that the species will also be found in Australian waters (Lowry & Stoddart, 2003).

Australian distribution. Western Australia: off the western coast (Pillai, 1973).

Australian geographic areas. South-western Australia; North-western Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Bay of Bengal; Andaman Sea; eastern Indian Ocean (Pillai, 1973).
