Neodoxomysis sahulensis Murano, 1999

Neodoxomysis sahulensis Murano, 1999: 356, figs 3, 4. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 453.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: male, 5.0 mm, NSMT Cr 12490. PARATYPES: 1 female, 4.0 mm, NSMT Cr 12491; 2 females, 3.7 mm, 4.0 mm, 1 male, NSMT Cr 12492.

Type locality. Sahul Shelf, west of Darwin, Northern Territory, Australia, (12°37.3'S 124°33.9'E to 12°36.0'S 124°36.4'E).

Description. Based on Murano, 1999: 356, figs 3, 4.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, narrowly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, longer than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins with setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent. Labrum symmetrical.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, with distinct carpus and propodus, terminal brush of setae present.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-4 biramous. Pleopod 3 endopod shorter than exopod. Pleopod 4 endopod with multiple articles, endopod shorter than exopod; exopod with 7 articles, second last article shorter than third last article, second last article slightly shorter than third last article. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, 34 setae from statocyst nearly to the apex of medial margin, slender setae present, exopod much longer than endopod; exopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, below diaresis. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, cleft or notched 20–30%, with two plumose setae, robust setae present along entire inner and outer margins.

Habitat. Littoral.

Australian distribution. Northern Territory: Sahul Shelf, west of Darwin (Murano, 1999).

Australian geographic areas. Northern Australia.
