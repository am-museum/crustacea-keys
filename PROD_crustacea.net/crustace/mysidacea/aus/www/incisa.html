Anisomysis incisa Tattersall, 1936

Anisomysis incisa Tattersall, 1936a: 161. --Tattersall, 1936b: 282. --Ii, 1964: 562, fig. 149. --Gordan, 1957: 340. --Bacescu, 1973: 179 (key). --Mauchline, 1980: 336. --Fenton, 1985c: 124, 128 (key). --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 457.

Type material. SYNTYPES BMNH.

Type locality. Low Islets Anchorage (as Low Isles), Great Barrier Reef, Queensland, Australia.

Description. Based on Tattersall, 1936a: 161.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, broadly rounded; spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale ratio of length x width 6.1 to 9:1, subequal in length to antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins with setae, spines on lateral margins absent, spines on medial margin absent, distally rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Body smooth, pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, pereopod 3 shorter than perepod 4, endopod slender, with carpus and propodus fused, terminal brush of setae absent.
Pleon. Pleopod 4 uniramous or greatly reduced. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, endopod and exopod subequal in length. Uropod exopod robust setae absent. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, cleft or notched, cleftness 20–30%, without plumose setae, robust setae on inner margin absent, robust setae on lateral margins present, on medial margin.

Habitat. Continental shelf, pelagic, sublittoral.

Depth zones. Littoral, or Upper bathyal.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Low Isles, Great Barrier Reef (Tattersall, 1936a); lagoon and Mrs Watsons Bay, Lizard Island, Great Barrier Reef (Fenton, 1985c; AM). Western Australia: Penguin Island, near Rockingham (AM).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia; south-western Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Iwayama Bay, Pelew Island (Ii, 1964).
