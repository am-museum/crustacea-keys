Haplostylus indicus (Hansen, 1910)

Gastrosaccus indicus Hansen, 1910: 56, pl. VIII, fig. 2A-R. --Tattersall, 1911: 125. --Illig, 1930: 568. --Delsman, 1939: 167. --Tattersall, 1940: 331, fig. 2A-H. --Dakin & Colefax, 1940: 132, fig. 223. --Tattersall, 1951: 90. --Gordan, 1957: 350. --Ii, 1964: 254; 581. Tattersall, 1960: 169. --Tattersall, 1965: 81. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 55. --Panampunnayil, 1989: 1313 (table). --Wang & Liu, 1987: 212, fig. 4. --Carleton & Hamner, 1989: 464, table 1. --Muller, 1993: 76.
Haplostylus (Gastrosaccus) indicus. --Fenton, 1985c: 56, 63 (key).
Haplostylus indicus. --Panampunnayil, 1989: 1313 (table). --Fenton, 1990b: 444, 449 (key). --Greenwood et al., 1991: 521 (table). --Panampunnayil, 1997: 1216 (table 1). --Hanamura, 1998: 154. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 435.

Type material. SYNTYPES: 16 specimens, ZMC, 26 specimens, ZMA.

Type locality. Indonesia: off North-Ubian (6°7.5'N 120°26'E), 16–23 m depth; off Pulu-Tongil, Sulu-Archipelago; off Sawan, Siau-Island, 27 m depth; Molucca's (0°17.6'S 129°14.5'E), 1000 m depth.

Description. Based on Hansen, 1910: 56, pl. VIII, fig. 2A-R.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, broadly rounded; spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale ratio of length x width 2:1 to 5:1, subequal in length to antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, spines on lateral margins absent, spines on medial margin absent, distally rounded, distal spine present, apical suture absent, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Body smooth.
Pleon. Pleopod 1 biramous. Pleopod 2 biramous. Pleopod 3 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopod 4 biramous, endopod with a single article, endopod subequal to exopod, exopod with 1 articles, exopod second last article third last article absent. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, 5–6 robust setae from statocyst nearly to the apex, slender setae present, endopod and exopod subequal in length. Uropod exopod diaresis absent, 13 robust setae present, slender setae on lateral margin absent. Telson longer than broad, distally acute or subacute, cleft or notched, cleftness less than 20%, without plumose setae, robust setae on inner margin present, robust setae along entire inner margin, robust setae on lateral margins present, on entire lateral margins.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Head rostrum narrowly rounded. Antenna 2 scale shorter than antenna 1 peduncle.

Habitat. Benthic, pelagic.

Depth zones. Littoral.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Casuarina Beach, Lizard Island, Great Barrier Reef, (AM); Davies Reef, Great Barrier Reef (Carleton & Hamner, 1989); Moreton Island (Hanamura, 1998). New South Wales: Port Stephens (Tattersall, 1940); Port Stephens and Broken Bay (Dakin & Colefax, 1940); Juno Head, Hawkesbury River (AM). Bass Strait: (Fenton, 1985c).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia, South-eastern Australia, Southern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. East Indies (Hansen, 1910); north-east of Madagascar, coast of India (Tattersall, 1911); Java (Delsman, 1939); South China Sea (Tattersall, 1960; Wang & Liu, 1987); Philippine Islands (Tattersall, 1951).
