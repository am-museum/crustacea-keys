Pseudanchialina inermis (Illig, 1906)

Chlamydopleon inerme Illig, 1906: 209, fig. 16.
Pseudanchialina inermis. --Hansen, 1910: 61, pl. IX, fig. 2a-d. --Fox, 1927: 199. --Tattersall, 1927: 188. --Illig, 1930: 422, fig. 40. --Coifmann, 1937: 31, pl. XV, figs 20a-d. --Gordan, 1957: 374. --Pillai, 1965: 1703, fig. 47. --Pillai, 1973: 75, fig. 37. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 72. --Sale et al., 1978: 133. --Valbonesi & Murano, 1980: 213. --Murano, 1980: 213, fig. 2. --Murano, 1983: 82, fig. 1. --Fenton, 1985c: 69 (key). --Carleton & Hamner, 1989: 464, table 1. --Murano, 1990: 195. --Muller, 1993: 69. --Fukuoka & Murano, 2002: 99 (key). --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 437.
Pseudanchialina erythraea Nouvel, 1944: 267.
Pseudanchialina sibogae Nouvel, 1959: 222.

Type material. SYNTYPES: ZMB cat No. 22 582.

Type locality. West of Sumatra; east of Sri Lanka (as Ceylon).

Description. Based on Illig, 1906: 209, fig. 16.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, narrowly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, shorter than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent. Labrum with medial process; medial process with setae.
Pereon. Body with extensive spination. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, terminal brush of setae present.
Pleon. Pleopod 1 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopods 2-3 biramous. Pleopod 3 endopod shorter than exopod. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, with 1, in statocyst region only, slender setae present, endopod and exopod subequal in length; exopod diaresis absent, 1 robust setae present, slender setae absent on lateral margin. Telson longer than broad, distally angled and convex, cleft or notched less than 20%, without plumose setae, robust setae absent along inner margin, present along distal third of outer margin.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami absent.

Habitat. Benthic, pelagic, sublittoral.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Davies Reef, Great Barrier Reef (Carleton & Hamner, 1989); Heron Reef, Great Barrier Reef (Sale et al., 1978; Fenton, 1985). Western Australia: off the north-western coast (Pillai, 1973).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia; North-western Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Indo West Pacific: Tanzania (Valbonesi & Murano, 1980); Bay of Bengal (Hansen, 1910); east of Sri Lanka and west of Sumatra (Illig, 1906); west of Thailand, south of Burma (Pillai, 1973); strait of Malacca (Pillai, 1973); south of Java, south-west coast of Sumatra, west of Sumatra, south of India (Pillai, 1973); between Nusi Besi and the north-east point of Timor (Hansen, 1910); east coast of Vietnam (Pillai, 1973); south of Taiwan (Pillai, 1973); Tanabe Bay, Japan (Valbonesi & Murano, 1980).
