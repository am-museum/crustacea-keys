Neognathophausia ingens (Dohrn, 1870)

Lophogaster ingens Dohrn, 1870: 610, pl. 31. --Willemoes-Suhm, 1876: 276. --Stebbing, 1893: 260
Gnathophausia inflata Willemoes-Suhm, 1873: in ms.
Gnathophausia ingens Sars, 1884: 4. --Sars, 1885: 30. --Gerstaecker & Ortmann, 1901: 602. --Hansen, 1912 : 184. --Tattersall, 1925: 2. --Hansen, 1927: 15. --Illig, 1930: 407, figs. 11–19. --Shiino, 1937: 181. --Tattersall, 1939: 224. --Fage, 1940: 336. --Legendre, 1940: 171. --Fage, 1941: 15, figs 20–25. --Nouvel, 1943: 9. --Tattersall, 1951: 25. --Banner, 1954: 98. --Bannet, 1954: 447. --Tattersall, 1955: 31, tab. 1. --Birstein & Tchindonova, 1962: 59. --Katman, et al., 1986: 160. --Escobar et al., 1991: 80, tab. 1 (biogeography). --Bacescu, 1991: 81, fig. 1 A-D
Gnathophausia calcarata Sars, 1884: 5. --Sars, 1885: 35. --Gerstaecker & Ortmann, 1901: 602. --Illig, 1905: 662. --Ortmann, 1906: 968. --Ortmann, 1906: 30. --Stebbing, 1908: 43. --Stebbing, 1910: 402. --Tattersall, 1912: 121. --Van Straelen, 1924: 437. --Zimmer, 1927: 633.
Gnathophausia bengalensis Wood-Mason & Alcock, 1891: 269.
Gnathophausia doryphora Illig, 1906: 227.
Neognatophausia ingens Petryashov, 1992: 47. --Muller, 1993: 6. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 426.

Type material. Type status and whereabouts unknown.

Type locality. Off the west coast of Africa.

Description. Based on Dohrn, 1870: 610.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, elongate, attenuated. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins with spines, distal spine present, apical suture absent, spine on outer distal corner present.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod broad, terminal brush of setae present.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-4 biramous. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, endopod and exopod subequal in length; exopod diaresis present, robust setae absent, slender setae present, on distal quarter of lateral martgin. Telson longer than broad, distally concave, entire, without plumose setae, robust setae along distal two thirds of lateral margin.

Habitat. Oceanic.

Australian distribution. Queensland: east of Cape York (11°57.17'S 145°40.34'E), 2495 m depth (AM). New South Wales: east of Port Kembla (34°20'S 151°56'E) (AM); east of Broken Bay, Tasman Sea (33°32'S 152°06' E) (AM); south-east of Montague Island (36°24'S 150°18'E) (AM); east of Port Hacking, Tasman Sea (34°05'S 151°55'E), 950 m depth (AM); south-east of Green Cape (37°24'S 150°30'E) (AM); south-east of Newcastle (33°07'S 151°11'E), 641 m depth (AM); east of Brush Island (35°36'S 150°55'E), 549 m (AM); off Norah Head (33°05'S 153°15'E to 33°20'S 152°32'E) (AM); south-east of Eden (37°14'S 150°22'E), 348 m (AM); Lord Howe Rise, Tasman Sea (27°11.97'S 160°37.8'E), 1960 m depth (AM). Tasmania: Tasmanian coast (Lowry & Stoddart, 2003).

Australian geographic areas. South-eastern Australia, Southern Australia, South-western Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Bay of Bengal (Wood-Mason & Alcock, 1891); Cocos (Keeling) Islands (Lowry & Stoddart, 2003); Talaur Islands, south of Mindano, Philippines (Sars, 1885); Hawaiian Islands (Ortman, 1906); California (Tattersall, 1951); Gulf of Mexico (Ortman, 1906); off Cape Town, South Africa (Illig, 1906).
