Synerythrops intermedia Hansen, 1910

Synerythrops intermedia Hansen, 1910: 65, pl. 9, fig. 5A-E, pl. 10, fig. 1A-C. --Illig, 1930: 576 (key). --Tattersall, 1936a: 150, fig. 3. --Tattersall, 1939: 237, figs 11, 13. --Gordan, 1957: 382. --Ii, 1964: 342. --Pillai, 1965: 54. --Murano, 1975: 91, figs 4A, 5–6. --Fenton, 1985c: 78. --Liu & Wang, 1986: 171, fig. 6. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 445.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: female, ZMA.

Type locality. Manipa Strait, eastern Indonesia (03º20´S 127º22.9´E), 0–1536 m depth.

Description. Based on Hansen, 1910: 65, pl. 9, fig. 5A-E, pl. 10, fig. 1A-C.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, narrowly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, longer than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod.
Pleon. Pleopod 1 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopods 2-4 biramous. Pleopod 4 endopod with multiple articles. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, exopod much longer than endopod; exopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, below diaresis. Telson longer than broad, distally truncate, emarginate, with two plumose setae, robust setae absent.

Habitat. Continental slope, pelagic.

Australian distribution. Queensland: outside Trinity Opening, Great Barrier Reef (Tattersall, 1936a).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Gulf of Aden (Tattersall, 1939); Manipa Strait, eastern Indonesia (Hansen, 1910).
