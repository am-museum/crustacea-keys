Neomysis japonica Nakazawa, 1910

Neomysis japonica Nakazawa, 1910: 247, pl. 8, figs 2, 25. --Illig, 1930: 597 (key). --Tattersall, 1932: 116 (key). --Ii, 1936: 580. --Banner, 1948: 74 (key). --Tattersall, 1951: 194, fig. 76. --Gordan, 1957: 368. --Ii, 1964: 447–451, fig. 113. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 68. --Hutchings, 1983: 20, table 2. --Liu & Wang, 1986: 188, fig. 16. --Murano, 1986: 117. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 459.

Type material. SYNTYPES: whereabouts unknown (believed lost).

Type locality. Teisanbori, a canal near Sendai; the Gulf of Tokoyo, near the mouth of River Kiso, Prov. Ise; the Kojima Gulf in the Inland Sea.

Description. Based on Nakazawa, 1910: 247, pl. 8, figs 2, 25.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, narrowly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 10:1 to 15:1 x width, longer than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins with setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin pointed, distal spine absent, apical suture present.
Pereon. Body smooth. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, terminal brush of setae present.
Pleon. Pleopod 4 biramous; endopod with a single article, endopod shorter than exopod; exopod with 2 articles, second last article third last article absent. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, 35–37 setae, on entire medial margin, slender setae present; exopod diaresis absent. Telson longer than broad, distally acute or subacute, emarginate, without plumose setae.

Habitat. Estuarine.

Australian distribution. New South Wales: Hunter River (introduced from Japan, Hutchings, 1983; 1 specimen, AM P31333; many specimens, AM P31341; 1 specimen, AM P31334).

Australian geographic areas. South-eastern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Japan: Teisanbori, a canal near Sendai; the Gulf of Tokyo (Nakazawa, 1910); Kojima Gulf, Inland Sea (Nakazawa, 1910).
