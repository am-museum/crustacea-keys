Neodoxomysis littoralis (Tattersall, 1922)

Doxomysis littoralis Tattersall, 1922: 278, fig. 17A-E. --Illig, 1930: 582 (key). --Tattersall, 1936a: 154. --Tattersall, 1936b: 279. --Gordan, 1957: 346. --Ii, 1964: 381, fig. 97. --Ii, 1964: 381. --Pillai, 1965: 1717, fig. 77. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 53. --Fenton, 1985c: 95 (key). --Liu & Wang, 1986: 173, fig. 8. --Carleton & Hamner, 1989: 464, table 1.
Neodoxomysis littoralis. --Murano, 1999: 352. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 453.

Type material. SYNTYPES: whereabouts unknown (possibly in BMNH or ZSI).

Type locality. Channel north of Viper Island, Port Blair, Andaman Islands.

Description. Based on Tattersall, 1922: 278, fig. 17A-E.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, narrowly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 6.1 to 9:1 x width, longer than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins with setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner present.
Pereon. Body smooth. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, shorter than perepod 4, endopod slender, with carpus and propodus fused and subdivided.
Pleon. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, 32 setae from statocyst nearly to the apex of medial margin, slender setae present, exopod moderately longer than endopod; exopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, on entire lateral margin. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, cleft or notched more than 30%, with two plumose setae, robust setae present along entire inner and outer margins.

Habitat. Benthic, coral reef, sublittoral.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Lizard Island: lagoon, Casuarina Beach, between Bird Islet and Trawler Beach, Mrs Watsons Bay, (Fenton, 1985c); Davies Reef, Great Barrier Reef (Carleton & Hamner, 1989); Low Isles, Great Barrier Reef (Tattersall, 1936a); Heron Island (Fenton, 1985c). New South Wales: Hawkesbury River: near Hungry Beach Gentleman's Halt, Juno Head (AM).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia; South-eastern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Andaman Islands (Tattersall, 1922); South China Sea (Ii, 1964); Karimata Strait (as Carimata Strait), Indonesia (Ii, 1964).
