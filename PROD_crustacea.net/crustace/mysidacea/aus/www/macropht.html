Heteromysis (Olivemysis) macrophthalma Bacescu, 1983

Heteromysis (Olivemysis) macrophthalma Bacescu, 1983: 9, fig. 3. --Fenton, 1985c: 142, 146 (key). --Murano, 1988: 46 (key). --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 448.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: male, QM 9692 (apparently deposited under the name Heteromysis magnoculata, see Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 448). PARATYPE: 1 male, MGAB no. 553.

Type locality. Heron Island, Great Barrier Reef, Queensland.

Description. Based on Bacescu, 1983: 9, fig. 3.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk present; rostrum present, narrowly rounded. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, shorter than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins with setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent. Labrum symmetrical.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, with carpus and propodus fused and subdivided, terminal brush of setae absent.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-4 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopod 4 endopod with a single article. Uropod endopod robust setae present, 2–4 setae, in statocyst region only. Telson longer than broad, distally truncate, cleft or notched 20–30%, without plumose setae, robust setae present on proximal three quarters of inner margin, along entire outer margins with a median gap.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Heron Island, Great Barrier Reef (Bacescu, 1983).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia.
