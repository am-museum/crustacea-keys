Anchialina media Ii, 1964

Anchialina media Ii, 1964: 204, figs 52–54. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 46, tab. 1. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 432.

Type material. PARATYPES: 1 male, 2 juveniles, 7 females (Ii’s collection No. 113, lost).

Type locality. South China Sea: between Sumatra and Borneo (0°45'N 106°11'E); west of Hainan Island (18°00'N 108°00'E); between Malaya and Borneo (7°53'N 108°27'E and 6°50'N 108°27' E); off Lingaa Island (0°22'S 105°23'E and 0°44'S 106°41' E); between Sumatra and Borneo (1°38' S, 107°42' E); north of Java (4°12'S 109°10'E); south-west of Borneo (2°15'N 108°20'E).

Description. Based on Ii, 1964: 204, figs 52–54.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, triangular, acute, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, shorter than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, terminal brush of setae absent.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-3 biramous. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami bilobed. Pleopod 3 endopod shorter than exopod. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present on entire medial margin, slender setae present, endopod and exopod subequal in length; exopod diaresis absent, 15–19 robust setae present, slender setae absent on lateral margin. Telson longer than broad, distally angled and convex, cleft or notched less than 20%, without plumose setae, robust setae present along entire inner margin, along distal two thirds of lateral margin.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopod 2 uniramous or rudimentary, pseudobranchial rami absent.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Great Barrier Reef (Lowry & Stoddart, 2003).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. South China Sea (Ii, 1964).
