Siriella nodosa Hansen, 1910

Siriella nodosa Hansen, 1910: 33, pl. III, fig. 1a-l. --Colosi, 1919: 6. --Colosi, 1920: 229. --Illig, 1930: 561. --Tattersall, 1936a: 145. --Tattersall, 1936b: 279. --Tattersall, 1943: 65. --Gordan, 1957: 380. --Ii, 1964: 85. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 77. --Bacescu, 1979: 143. --Fenton, 1985c: 37, 47 (key). --Carleton & Hamner, 1989: 464, table 1. --Murano, 1990: 192, fig. 3. --Muller, 1993: 40. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 467.

Type material. SYNTYPES: 5 specimens, ZMA.

Type locality. Anchorage off Kawio and Karkaralong Islands, Karkaralong group, Siboga Station 129, 23–31 m depth.

Description. Based on Hansen, 1910: 38, pl. IV, figs 3a-k.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions; rostrum present, narrowly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, shorter in length to antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture absent, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, terminal brush of setae absent.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-2, 4 biramous. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami spirally coiled. Pleopod 4 endopod with multiple articles. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, from statocyst nearly to the apex of medial margin, endopod and exopod subequal in length; exopod diaresis present, 3–4 robust setae present, slender setae present, on distal quarter of lateral margin, below diaresis. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, entire, with two plumose setae, robust setae present along entire outer margins with a medial gap.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopod 4 uniramous or rudimentary; endopod with a single article.

Habitat. Coral reef, pelagic, sublittoral.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Torres Strait (Colosi, 1919; Colosi, 1920); Davies Reef, Great Barrier Reef (Carleton & Hamner, 1989); Low Isles, Great Barrier Reef (Tattersall, 1936a); Heron Island, Great Barrier Reef (Bacescu, 1979; Fenton, 1985).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Eastern Indonesia: Kawio and Karkaralong Islands, Karkaralong group (Hansen, 1910). Guam (Tattersall, 1943). Ryukyu Islands, Japan (Murano, 1990).
