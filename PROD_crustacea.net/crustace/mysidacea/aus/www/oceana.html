Katerythrops oceanae Holt & Tattersall, 1905

Katerythrops oceanae Holt & Tattersall, 1905: 107, pl. 20, 6 figs. --Holt & Tattersall, 1905: 117, 143. --Holt & Tattersall, 1905: 8. --Hansen, 1905: 7. --Holt & Tattersall, 1906a: 24. --Illig, 1906: 198, fig. 5a-b. --Zimmer, 1909: 93, figs 188–191. --Tattersall, 1911: 30. --Tattersall, 1926: 10. --Illig, 1930: 432: figs 55, 56. -- --Hansen, 1938: 166. --Nouvel, 1943: 77. --Tattersall, 1951: 118. --Tattersall & Tattersall, 1951: 214, fig. 47a-g. --Tattersall, 1955: 118. --Gordan, 1957: 354. --Ii, 1964: 307, fig. 80. --Murano, 1976: 35, figs 9–13, table 3. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 61. --Mauchline, 1980: 329. --Fenton, 1985c: 77. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 445.
Katerythrops dactylops Stebbing, 1910: 404.
Katerythrops sp. Tattersall, 1936a: 21.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: male, BMNH.

Type locality. South-west of Ireland (52º27´6"N 15º40´W or 52º20´N 15º7´9"W), Atlantic Ocean.

Description. Based on Holt & Tattersall, 1905: 107, pl. 20, 6 figs.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, emarginate, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 6.1 to 9:1 x width, shorter than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture absent, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Body smooth. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, shorter than perepod 4, endopod slender, terminal brush of setae absent.
Pleon. Pleopod 1-4 biramous. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, exopod moderately longer then endopod; exopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, on entire lateral margin. Telson longer than broad, distally truncate, entire, without plumose setae, robust setae absent.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopod 1, 3 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopod 2 biramous. Pleopod 4 endopod with a single article.

Habitat. Bathypelagic, continental slope.

Australian distribution. Queensland: outside Trinity Opening, Great Barrier Reef (Tattersall, 1936a). Western Australia: west of Perth (Tattersall, 1955).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia, south-western Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Indian Ocean (Hansen, 1905; Tattersall, 1955); Atlantic Ocean: north and south Atlantic Ocean (Hansen, 1905); western Atlantic Ocean (Illig, 1906); near Bermuda and Bahama Islands (Tattersall, 1926); west coast of Ireland (Holt and Tattersall, 1905); Azores and Canary Islands (Hansen, 1905); South Africa (Zimmer, 1912); mouth of Congo, off Benguela and New Amsterdam (Illig, 1930); Pacific Ocean: off Hashima, near Atami, Sagami Bay, Japan (Ii, 1964).
