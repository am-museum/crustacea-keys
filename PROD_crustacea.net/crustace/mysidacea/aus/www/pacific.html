Haplostylus pacificus (Hansen, 1912)

Gastrosaccus pacificus Hansen, 1912: 198, pl. 2, figs 3A-G. --Tattersall, 1922: 461, fig. 8a-b. --Illig, 1930: 568 (key). --Tattersall, 1951: 90. --Tattersall, 1952: 176 (key). --Gordan, 1957: 350. --Ii, 1964: 255. --Pillai, 1965: 1699, fig. 31. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 56. --Panumpunnayil, 1989: 1313 (table).
Haplostylus (Gastrosaccus) pacificus. --Fenton, 1985c: 56, 63 (key).
Haplostylus pacificus. --Bácescu, 1979: 143. --Panaumpunnayil, 1989: 1313 (table). --Murano, 1990: 195. --Fenton, 1990b: 449 (key). --Greenwood et al., 1991: 521 (table). --Fukuoka & Murano, 1997: 525. --Hanamura, 1998: 168 (key). --Fukuoka & Murano, 2002: 65, 100 (key). --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 436.

Type material. SYNTYPES: USNM 000456360.

Type locality. Butaritan and Kiribati (as Gilbert Islands), on surface.

Description. Based on Hansen, 1912: 198, pl. 2, figs 3A-G.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, narrowly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, shorter than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture absent, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-2, 4 biramous. Pleopod 4 endopod with a single article, endopod subequal to exopod; exopod with 1 articles, second last article pleopod reduced. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, from statocyst nearly to the apex of medial margin, slender setae present, endopod and exopod subequal in length; exopod diaresis absent, 11–12 robust setae present, slender setae absent on lateral margin. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, cleft or notched less than 20%, without plumose setae, robust setae present along entire inner and outer margins.

Habitat. Pelagic.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Heron Island (Bácescu, 1979); Heron and Lizard Islands, Great Barrier Reef (Fenton, 1985).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Andaman Islands (Tattersall, 1922); Andaman Sea (Fukuoka & Murano, 2002); Philippine Islands (Tattersall, 1951); Kiribati (as Gilbert Islands) (Hansen, 1912); Ryukyu Islands, Japan (Murano, 1990; Fukuoka & Murano, 1997).
