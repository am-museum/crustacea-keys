Australerythrops paradicei Tattersall, 1928

Australerythrops paradicei Tattersall, 1928: 107, figs 29–30. --Illig, 1930: 576. --Gordan, 1957: 341. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 48. --Mauchline, 1980: 327. --Fenton, 1985c: 72. --O'Brien, 1989: 5. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 443.

Type material. SYNTYPES: BMNH.

Type locality. Watson's Bay, Port Jackson, New South Wales, Australia.

Description. Based on Tattersall, 1928: 107, figs 29–30.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, broadly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, longer than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin truncated, distal spine present, apical suture absent, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Body smooth. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, terminal brush of setae absent.
Pleon. Pleopod 1 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopods 2-4 biramous. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami straight. Pleopod 4 endopod with multiple articles, endopod longer than exopod; exopod with 10 articles, second last article subequal to third last article. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, 23 robust setae present from statocyst nearly to the apex of medial margin, slender setae present, exopod moderately longer than endopod; exopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, on entire lateral margin. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, entire, without plumose setae, robust setae present along distal half of outer margin.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami absent.

Habitat. Littoral.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Great Barrier Reef (AM). New South Wales: Watson's Bay, Port Jackson (Tattersall, 1928). Tasmania: Eagle Neck, Clydes Island (Fenton, 1985c).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia, South-eastern Australia, Southern Australia.
