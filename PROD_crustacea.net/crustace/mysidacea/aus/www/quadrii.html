Siriella quadrispinosa Hansen, 1910

Siriella quadrispinosa Hansen, 1910: 32, pl. II, fig. 5a-I. --Tattersall, 1922: 454. --Illig, 1930: 561. --Gordan, 1957: 381. --Ii, 1964: 91, fig. 22. --Pillai, 1965: 1691, fig. 14. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 77. Udrescu, 1981: xx. --Fenton, 1985c: 37, 48 (key). --Carleton & Hamner, 1989: 464, table 1. --Murano, 1990: 193, fig. 4. --Muller, 1993: 42. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 467.

Type material. SYNTYPES: 17 specimens, ZMC, many specimens, ZMA.

Type locality. Bank between Bahuluwang and Tambolungan Islands, south of Saleyer, 8–10 m depth, Siboga Station 66.

Description. Based on Hansen, 1910: 32, pl. II, fig. 5a-I.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, broadly rounded or narrowly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, shorter than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-2, 4 biramous. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami spirally coiled. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present from statocyst nearly to the apex of medial margin, slender setae present, endopod and exopod subequal in length; exopod diaresis present, 6–9 robust setae present, slender setae present, on distal quarter of lateral margin, below diaresis. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, entire, with two plumose setae, robust setae present along entire outer margins with a median gap.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami absent.

Habitat. Coral reef, pelagic, sublittoral.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Davies Reef, Great Barrier Reef (Carleton & Hamner, 1989); Heron Island, Great Barrier Reef (Fenton, 1985).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Gulf of Manaar, India (Tattersall, 1922). Eastern Indonesia (Hansen, 1910). Amami-Oshima Island and Izu Peninsula, Japan (Ii, 1964); Ryukyu Islands, Japan (Murano, 1990). Bennet Island, Chesterfield Islands, Coral Sea (Bacescu, 1981).
