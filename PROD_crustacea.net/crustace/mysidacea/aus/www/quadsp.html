Heteromysis (Olivemysis) quadrispinosa Murano, 1988

Heteromysis (Olivemysis) quadrispinosa Murano, 1988: 34, fig. 8. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 449.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: female, NTM Cr.005494/A. PARATYPE: male, NTM Cr.005494/B.

Type locality. Table Head, Port Essington, Northern Territory, Australia (11°14.8'S 132°11.2'E), 4 m depth.

Description. Based on Murano, 1988: 34, fig. 8.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk present; rostrum present, triangular, acute, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, shorter than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins with setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Body smooth. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod broad, with carpus and propodus fused, terminal brush of setae absent.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-4 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopod 4 endopod with a single article. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, 4 setae in statocyst region only, slender setae present, exopod moderately longer than endopod; exopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, on entire lateral margin. Telson longer than broad, distally truncate, cleft or notched 20–30%, without plumose setae, robust setae present along entire inner and outer margins.

Habitat. Benthic, coral rubble.

Australian distribution. Northern Territory: Table Head, Port Essington (Murano, 1988).

Australian geographic areas. Northern Australia.
