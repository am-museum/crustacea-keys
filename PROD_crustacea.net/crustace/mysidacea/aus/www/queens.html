Haplostylus queenslandiensis  (Bacescu & Udrescu, 1982)

Gastrosaccus queenslandiensis Bacescu & Udrescu, 1982: 86, fig. 3. --Panampunnayil, 1989: 1313 (table)
Haplostylus (Gastrosaccus) queenslandiensis. --Fenton, 1985c: 56, 64 (key).
Haplostylus queenslandiensis. --Fenton, 1990b: 450 (key). --Greenwood et al., 1991: 520, figs 4–7. --Woodridge et al., 1992: 168 (table). --Muller, 1998: 84. --Panampunnayil, 1997: 1232 (table). --Hanamura, 1998: 157, fig. 4. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 436.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: male, QM W8715.

Type locality. Mouth of Brisbane River, Queensland, Australia, 1–2 m depth.

Description. Based on Bacescu & Udrescu, 1982: 86, fig. 3.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, narrowly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, shorter than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent. Labrum with medial process; medial process without setae.
Pereon. Body smooth. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, shorter than perepod 4, endopod slender, with carpus and propodus fused and subdivided, terminal brush of setae absent.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-4 biramous. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami triangular. Pleopod 3 endopod shorter than exopod. Pleopod 4 endopod with a single article, endopod shorter than exopod; exopod with 6 articles. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, 5 setae from statocyst nearly to the apex of medial margin, slender setae present or plumose setae present, exopod moderately longer then endopod; exopod diaresis absent, 12 robust setae present, slender setae absent on lateral margin. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, cleft or notched 20–30%, without plumose setae, robust setae present along entire inner and outer margins.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopods 2-4 uniramous or rudimentary.

Australian distribution. Queensland: mouth of Brisbane River (Bacescu & Udrescu, 1982); Pumicestone Passage in northern Moreton Bay; Wild Cattle Creek near Gladstone (Greenwood et al., 1992).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia.
