Paranchialina secunda Panampunnayil, 1986

Paranchialina secunda Panampunnayil, 1986: 1183, figs 1–26. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 437.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: 1 male, IOBC-0388–10–50–1985. PARATYPES: 1 female, IOBC-0388A-10–50–1985, 3 males, 3 females, IOBC-0388B-10–50–1985.

Type locality. Off Western Australian coast between Busselton and Albany, Australia (33° - 35°S to 114° - 119°E), about 40 m depth.

Description. Based on Panampunnayil, 1986: 1183, figs 1–26.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, narrowly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, shorter than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent. Labrum with medial process; medial process with setae.
Pereon. Body with extensive spination. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, with carpus and propodus fused and subdivided, terminal brush of setae present.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-4 biramous. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami straight. Pleopod 3 endopod shorter than exopod. Pleopod 4 endopod with multiple articles, endopod subequal to exopod; exopod with 7 articles, second last article subequal to third last article. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present on entire medial margin, slender setae present, endopod moderately longer than exopod; exopod diaresis absent, 2 robust setae present, slender setae present, on distal quarter of lateral margin. Telson longer than broad, angled and convex, cleft or notched 20–30%, without plumose setae, robust setae present along entire inner margin, along distal two thirds of lateral margin.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopod 1-4 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami triangular. Pleopod 4 endopod with a single article.

Habitat. Pelagic.

Australian distribution. Western Australia: off south-western coast between Busselton and Albany (Panumpunnayil, 1986).

Australian geographic areas. South-eastern Australia.
