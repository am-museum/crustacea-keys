Anisomysis spatulispina Murano, 1995

Anisomysis spatulispina Murano, 1995: 145, fig. 1. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 459.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: male, NTM Cr.004319.

Type locality. Coral Bay, Port Essington, Northern Territory, Australia (11°11.3'S 132°11.5'E), 4 m depth.

Description. Based on Murano, 1995: 145, fig. 1.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, narrowly rounded; spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale ratio of length x width 6.1 to 9:1, longer than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins with setae, spines on lateral margins absent, spines on medial margin absent, distally rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, with carpus and propodus fused, terminal brush of setae absent.
Pleon. Pleopod 4 biramous, endopod with a single article, endopod shorter than exopod, exopod with 4 articles, exopod second last article longer than third last article, exopod second last article slightly longer than third last article. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae present, exopod much longer than endopod. Uropod exopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae on lateral margin present, slender setae on lateral margin on entire margin. Telson longer than broad, distally concave, cleft or notched, cleftness 20–30%, without plumose setae, robust setae on inner margin present, robust setae along entire inner margin, robust setae on lateral margins present, on distal third of lateral margin.

Habitat. Sublittoral.

Depth zones. Littoral.

Australian distribution. Northern Territory: Coral Bay, Port Essington (Murano, 1995).

Australian geographic areas. Northern Australia.
