Doxomysis spinata Murano, 1990

Doxomysis spinata Murano, 1990: 235, figs 3–4. --Talbot, 1997: 432, fig. 4. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 452.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: male, NTM Cr.006760.

Type locality. Table Head, Port Essington, Northern Territory, Australia, 8 m depth.

Description. Based on Murano, 1990: 235, figs 3–4.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, narrowly rounded; spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale ratio of length x width 2:1 to 5:1, longer than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins with setae, spines on lateral margins absent, spines on medial margin absent, distally rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner present. Labrum symmetrical.
Pereon. Body smooth. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, with distinct carpus and propodus, terminal brush of setae absent.
Pleon. Pleopod 1 biramous. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami straight. Pleopod 3 biramous. Pleopod 4 biramous, endopod with multiple articles, endopod shorter than exopod, exopod with 7 articles, exopod second last article subequal to third last article. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, 21–37 robust setae from statocyst nearly to the apex, exopod much longer than endopod. Uropod exopod diaresis absent, robust setae absent, slender setae on lateral margin present, slender setae on lateral margin on entire margin. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, cleft or notched, cleftness 20–30%, with two plumose setae, robust setae on inner margin present, robust setae along entire inner margin, robust setae on lateral margins present, on entire lateral margins.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami absent.

Habitat. Sublittoral.

Depth zones. Littoral.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Lizard Island, Great Barrier Reef (Talbot, 1997). Northern Territory: Table Head, Port Essington (Murano, 1990).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia, Northern Australia.
