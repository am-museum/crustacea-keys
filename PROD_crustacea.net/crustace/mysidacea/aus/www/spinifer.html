Hypererythrops spinifera (Hansen, 1910)

Erythrops spinifera Hansen, 1910: 62, pl. 9, fig. 3A-E. --Gordan, 1957: 354.
Hyperythrops spinifera. --Tattersall, 1922: 464, fig. 11. --Illig, 1930: 575 (key). --Tattersall, 1936a: 150. --Tattersall, 1936b: 281. Gordan, 1957: 354. --Ii, 1964: 328, fig. 85. --Pillai, 1964: 24. --Pillai, 1965: 1705. --Tattersall, 1965: 89. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 61. --Valbonesi & Murano, 1980: 215. --Fenton, 1985c: 76. --Liu & Wang, 1986: 164, fig. 3. --Muller, 1993: 117. --Wang & Liu, 1994: 96. --Wang & Liu, 1997: 210. --Fukuoka & Murano, 2002: 73, 101 (key). --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 444.

Type material. SYNTYPES: 2 specimens, ZMA.

Type locality. Ceram Sea, West Papua New Guinea (02º28.5´S 131º3.3´E).

Description. Based on Hansen, 1910: pl. 9, fig. 3A-E.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, triangular, acute, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, longer than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Body smooth. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender, with carpus and propodus fused and subdivided, terminal brush of setae present.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-2 biramous. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami absent. Uropod endopod diaresis absent; exopod diaresis absent. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, entire, with two plumose setae, robust setae present along entire outer margins.

Habitat. Benthic, continental shelf, sublittoral.

Australian distribution. Queensland: outside Trinity Opening and Reef Lagoon, Great Barrier Reef (Tattersall, 1936a).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Arabian Sea (Pillai, 1964); Port Blair, Andaman Islands (Tattersall, 1922); Andaman Islands (Fukuoka & Murano, 2002); Strait of Malacca (Tattersall, 1965); South China Sea (Liu & Wang, 1986; Wang & Liu, 1994); East China Sea (Wang & Liu, 1997); Ceram Sea, West Papua New Guinea (Hansen, 1910); Tsushima Strait, Japan (Ii, 1964); Tanabe Bay, Japan (Valbonesi & Murano, 1980).
