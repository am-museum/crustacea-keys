Heteromysis (Heteromysis) spinosa Bacescu, 1986

Heteromysis spinosa Bacescu, 1986: 19–22, fig. 1A-L.
Heteromysis (Heteromysis) spinosa. --Murano, 1988: 29, fig. 2. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 447.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: 1 juv. female, NTM.

Type locality. Channel Island, Port Darwin, Northern Territory, Australia.

Description. Based on Bacescu, 1986: 19–22, fig. 1A-L.
Head. Eyes stalked, head with visual elements, head ommatidia not divided into separate regions, head spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; head rostrum present, head narrowly rounded; head rostrum spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale ratio of length x width 2:1 to 5:1, shorter than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, spines on lateral margins absent, spines on medial margin absent, distally rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Body smooth. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, terminal brush of setae present.
Pleon. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, 6 robust setae from statocyst nearly to the apex, endopod, endopod slender setae present. Uropod exopod diaresis absent, slender setae on lateral margin present, slender setae on lateral margin below diaresis. Telson longer than broad, distally truncate, telson cleft or notched, cleftness 20–30%, telson without plumose setae, robust setae on inner margin present, robust setae on the proximal quarter of inner margin only, robust setae on lateral margins present, telson on distal half of lateral margin.

Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleon. Pleopod 1 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopod 2 uniramous or rudimentary, pseudobranchial rami triangular. Pleopod 3 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopod 4 uniramous or greatly reduced, with a single article.

Habitat. Coral reef, ectocomensal, littoral, from pools, rubble and commensal with sponges.

Depth zones. Littoral.

Australian distribution. Northern Territory: Channel Island, Port Darwin (Bacescu, 1986); Table Head, Port Essington (11°14.8'S 132°11.2'E), 6 m depth Murano (1998); Fort Hill Wharf, Darwin Harbour, 5 m depth (Murano, 1988). Western Australia: Ashmore Reef (Murano, 1988).

Australian geographic areas. North-western Australia, or Northern Australia.
