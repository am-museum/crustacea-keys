Heteromysis (Gnathomysis) stellata Bacescu & Bruce, 1980

Heteromysis (Gnathomysis) stellata Bacescu & Bruce, 1980: 65–67, fig, 2a-l. --Fenton, 1985c: 142, 146 (key). --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 446.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: male, MGAB no. 575. PARATYPE: 1 FEMALE, MGAB no. 576.

Type locality. Southern reef edge, Heron Island, Queensland, Australia.

Description. Based on Bacescu & Bruce, 1980: 65–67, fig, 2a-l.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, triangular, acute, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, lateral margins with setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine absent, apical suture absent, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a gnathopod, endopod very broad, with carpus and propodus fused, terminal brush of setae present.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-4 uniramous or rudimentary. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami triangular. Pleopod 4 endopod with a single article. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, 15–17 setae from statocyst nearly to the apex of medial margin, slender setae present. Telson longer than broad, distally truncate, cleft or notched 20–30%, without plumose setae, robust setae present along entire inner margin, along distal half of outer margin.

Habitat. Coral reef, ectocommensal, algal crest, 'commensal with hermit crab Aniculus sp.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Heron Island, Great Barrier Reef (Bacescu & Bruce, 1980).

Australian geographic areas. South-eastern Australia.
