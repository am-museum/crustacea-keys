Gibberythrops stephensoni (Tattersall, 1936)

Metamblyops stephensoni Tattersall, 1936a: 152, fig. 4. --Gordan, 1957: 358. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 64.
Eoerythrops amamiensis Murano, 1976: 20, figs 1–2. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 54.
Gibberythrops stephensoni. --Murano, 1981: 273. --Fenton, 1985c: 75. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 444.

Type material. PARATYPES: BMNH.

Type locality. Outside Trinity Opening, Great Barrier Reef, Queensland, Australia.

Description. Based on Tattersall, 1936a: 152, fig. 4.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements; rostrum absent. Antenna 2 scale length 6.1 to 9:1 x width, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner present.
Pleon. Pleopods 2-4 biramous. Pleopod 3 endopod subequal to exopod. Pleopod 4 endopod with multiple articles, endopod subequal to exopod. Uropod endopod 2 robust setae present, in statocyst region only. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, entire, without plumose setae, robust setae present along distal half of lateral margin.

Habitat. Continental shelf.

Australian distribution. Queensland: outside Trinity Opening, Great Barrier Reef (Tattersall, 1936a).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Amami Islands, south-western Japan (Murano, 1976).
