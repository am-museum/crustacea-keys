Haplostylus tenuicaudus Hanamura, 1998

Haplostylus tenuicaudus Hanamura, 1998: 160, figs 5–8. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 436.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: 1 male, 7.5 mm, WAM 636–97. PARATYPES: 2 males, 7.3, 7.6 mm, and 3 ovigerous females, 6.8–9.1 mm, WAM 637–97; 2 males 7.3–7.5 mm WAM 638–97; 1 female, 4.5 mm, and 1 damaged male, WAM 402–95.

Type locality. Port Robinson, Nickol Bay, Western Australia (20°39'S 117°03'E).

Description. Based on Hanamura, 1998: 160, figs 5–8.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, narrowly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, shorter than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent. Labrum with medial process; medial process without setae.
Pereon. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender.
Pleon. Pleopods 1-4 biramous. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami triangular. Pleopod 3 endopod shorter than exopod. Pleopod 4 endopod with a single article, endopod shorter than exopod; exopod with 4 articles, second last article subequal to third last article. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present, 6 setae from statocyst nearly to the apex of medial margin, slender setae present, exopod moderately longer then endopod; exopod diaresis absent, 11 robust setae present, slender setae absent on lateral margin. Telson longer than broad, distally truncate, cleft or notched less than 20%, without plumose setae, robust setae present along entire inner and outer margins.
Female (sexually dimorphic characters). Pleopods 2, 4 uniramous or rudimentary.

Habitat. Benthic, planktonic.

Australian distribution. Western Australia: Nickol Bay; Barrow Island; Nornalup (all Hanamura, 1998).

Australian geographic areas. South-western Australia to North-western Australia.
