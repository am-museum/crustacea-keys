Anchialina typica orientalis Nouvel, 1971

Anchialina typica. --Hansen, 1910: 52, pl. VII, fig. 2a-k. --Hansen, 1912: 196. --Colosi, 1919: 7. --Colosi, 1920: 229. --Tattersall, 1922: 457. --Tattersall, 1923: 282. --Tattersall, 1926: 9. --Zimmer, 1927: 610. --Illig, 1930: 565. --Tattersall, 1936a: 148. --Tattersall, 1936b: 96. --Delsman, 1939: 166. --Nouvel, 1943: 70. --Tattersall, 1951: 100. --Tattersall, 1955: 89, fig. 15. --Tattersall, 1955: 183. --Gordan, 1957: 340. --Tattersall, 1960: 175. --Tattersall, 1962: 230. -- Ii, 1964: 188, figs. 48–49; 579. --Pillai, 1964: 18, fig. 10. --Pillai,1965: 1700, figs. 32–34. --Tattersall, 1965: 82. -- Brattegard, 1970: 24, fig. 6. --Fenton, 1985c: 52, 55 (key).
Anchialina typica orientalis Nouvel, 1971: 328, figs 2–9. --Pillai, 1973: 69, fig. 32. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 47, tab. 1. --Fukuoka & Murano, 1997: 523, fig. 3E. Fukuoka & Murano, 2002: 61. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 433.


Type material. SYNTYPES: whereabout unknown.

Type locality. Nosy-Bé madagascar.

Description. Based on Hansen, 1910: 52, pl. VII, fig. 2a-k.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, emarginate, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale shorter than antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture present.
Pereon. Body smooth. Pereopod 3 forming a pereopod, endopod slender.
Pleon. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami semicircular. Pleopod 3 biramous; endopod longer than exopod. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present on entire medial margin, slender setae present, endopod moderately longer than exopod; exopod diaresis absent, robust setae present. Telson longer than broad, distally acute or subacute, cleft or notched 20–30%, without plumose setae, robust setae present along entire inner margin, along distal two thirds of lateral margin.

Habitat. Hypoplanktonic; fine and medium sand; silty bottom; associated with corals, sponges, and artificial substrates; in plankton; weeds.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Low Isles, Great Barrier Reef (Tattersall, 1936a). Western Australia: south west coast (Pillai, 1973).

Australian geographic areas: North-eastern Australia; South-western Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Nosy-Bé madagascar  (Nouvel, 1971); Sailus Ketjil, Pulau Satengar (as Paternoster Islands) (Hansen, 1910); Pulu Kawassang, Pulau Satengar (as Paternoster Islands) (Hansen, 1910); off Laiwui, Pulau Satengar (as Paternoster Islands) (Hansen, 1910); east of Sailus Besar, Pulau Satengar (as Paternoster Islands) (Hansen, 1910); Bay of Kankamaraän, south coast of Kangeang (Hansen, 1910); off North-Ubian (Hansen, 1910); off Pulu Tongil, Sulu Archipelago (Hansen, 1910); Gulf of Thailand (Hansen 1910); Singapore Strait (Tattersall, 1960). South China Sea (Ii, 1964); Andaman Sea (Fukuoka & Murano, 2002); Japan (Fukuoka & Murano, 1997).
