Siriella vulgaris Hansen, 1910

Siriella suluhensis Czerniavsky, 1882: 108. --Czerniavsky, 1887: 29.
Siriella vulgaris Hansen, 1910: 34, pl. III, fig. 2a-k. --Tattersall, 1922: 454. --Colosi, 1924: 3. --Tattersall, 1928: 105. --Illig, 1930: 562. --Tattersall, 1936a: 145. --Tattersall, 1936b: 279. --Coifmann, 1937: 3. --Tattersall, 1943: 65. --Tattersall, 1951: 62. --Tattersall, 1951: 63, fig. 14. --Gordan, 1957: 381. --Tattersall, 1960: 166. --Ii, 1964: 94; 577. --Ii, 1964: 95. --Pillai, 1965: 1692, fig. 15. --Tattersall, 1965: 76. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 77. --Fenton, 1985c: 37, 49 (key). --Bacescu, 1986: 19. --Murano, 1990: 193, fig. 5. --Muller, 1993: 45. --Fukuoka & Murano, 1997: 521, fig. 2F, G. --Fukuoka & Murano, 2002: 59, 98 (key). --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 469.

Type material. SYNTYPES: many specimens, ZMC, many speciemns, ZMA.

Type locality. Indonesia: Bay of Kankamaraän, south coast of Kangeang (6°59'S 115°24.7'E), 22 m depth; north-east of Borneo (7°38'S 117°31'E), 640 m depth; north of Borneo (7°38'S 117°31'E), 10–0 m depth; Sailus Ketjil, Pulau Satengar (as Paternoster Islands), 27 m depth; between Bahluwang and Tambolugan islands, south of Saleyer, 8–10 m depth; Makassar, 27–32 m depth; east of Borneo (4°57.4'S 119°2.8'E), 11–0 m depth; off Sawan, Siau island, 27 m depth; off Kawai and Kamboling islands, Karkaralong group, 23–31 m depth; north-east of Celebes (5°56.7' N 126°25'E); off Lawai, Palau Obi (as Obi Major), 23 m depth; between Gisser and Ceram Laut, 18 m depth; off Kilsuin, west coast of Kur Island, 18 m depth; west side of Taam Island, 9–36 m depth; between Nusa Besi and the north-east point of Timor, 27–54 m depth. Philippines: south-east of Pearl Bank, 15m depth, off Kapul Island, 13 m depth, off Pulu Tongil, 13 m depth, Sulu Archipelago; off North Ubian (6°7.5'N 120°26'E), 16–23 m depth; Bay of Batjan, Batan Island; north-east side of Daram Island, 49 m depth.

Description. Based on Hansen, 1910: 34, pl. III, fig. 2a-k.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements, ommatidia not divided into separate regions, spine at anterodistal corner of eye stalk absent; rostrum present, triangular, acute, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture present, spine on outer distal corner absent.
Pleon. Pleopod 2 pseudobranchial rami spirally coiled. Pleopod 3 biramous. Uropod endopod diaresis absent, robust setae present on entire medial margin, endopod and exopod subequal in length; exopod diaresis present, 3–9 robust setae present, slender setae present, on distal quarter of lateral margin, below diaresis. Telson longer than broad, distally rounded, entire, with two plumose setae, robust setae present along entire outer margins with a median gap.

Habitat. Littoral, pelagic.

Australian distribution. Queensland: Owen Channel, Flinders Island, Princess Charlotte Bay (Tattersall, 1928); Low Isles, Great Barrier Reef (Tattersall, 1936a); Middle Banks, Moreton Bay (QM). Northern Territory: Channel Island, Darwin Harbour (Bacescu, 1986).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia; Northern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Arabian Sea (Colosi, 1924). India (Tattersall, 1922). Andaman Sea (Fukuoka & Murano, 2002). Singapore Strait (Tattersall, 1960); Hong Kong, South China Sea (Coifmann, 1937). Taiwan (Tattersall, 1951). Eastern Indonesia (Hansen, 1910). Philippine Islands (Hansen, 1910; Coifmann, 1937). Boso Peninsula, Suruga Bay, Japan (Ii, 1964); Ryukyu Islands, Japan (Murano, 1990; Fukuoka & Murano, 1997). Samoa and Guam (Tattersall, 1943).
