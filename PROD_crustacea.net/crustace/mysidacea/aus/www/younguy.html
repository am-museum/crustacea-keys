Erythrops yongei Tattersall, 1936

Erythrops yongei Tattersall, 1936a: 149, fig. 2. --Tattersall, 1951: 111–112. --Gordan, 1957: 348. --Ii, 1964: 355. --Mauchline & Murano, 1977: 54. --Mauchline, 1980: 328. --Fenton, 1985c: 74. --Lowry & Stoddart, 2003: 443.

Type material. HOLOTYPE: female, BMNH.

Type locality. Outside Papuan Pass, Great Barrier Reef, Queensland, Australia, 400 m depth.

Description. Based on Tattersall, 1936a: 149, fig. 2.
Head. Eyes stalked, with visual elements; rostrum present, narrowly rounded, spines on lateral margins absent. Antenna 2 scale length 2:1 to 5:1 x width, subequal in length to antenna 1 peduncle, lateral margins without setae, without spines, medial margins without spines, distal margin rounded, distal spine present, apical suture present.
Pereon. Body smooth.
Pleon. Telson about as long as broad, distally truncate, entire, without plumose setae, robust setae absent

Habitat. Continental shelf, pelagic.

Australian distribution. Queensland: outside Papuan Pass, Great Barrier Reef (Tattersall, 1936a).

Australian geographic areas. North-eastern Australia.

Extrinsic distribution. Philippine Islands (Tattersall, 1951).
